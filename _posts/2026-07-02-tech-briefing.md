## Top Stories

**Rocket Lab agreed to acquire satellite operator Iridium in an $8 billion cash-and-stock deal, the largest space-sector merger of 2026.** Iridium holders will receive $54 per share — $27 in cash plus Rocket Lab stock — a roughly 24% premium to the prior close, valuing the target at about $8.0 billion enterprise value. The transaction fuses Rocket Lab's launch vehicles and satellite manufacturing with Iridium's global L-band network, licensed spectrum, and more than 2.5 million subscribers across defense, aviation, maritime, and commercial markets, creating a vertically integrated challenger to SpaceX's Starlink. Rocket Lab shares popped roughly 16% and Iridium jumped about 25% on the news; the deal is expected to close in mid-2027 ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/29/rocket-lab-buys-iridium.html), [Rocket Lab](https://investors.rocketlabcorp.com/news-releases/news-release-details/rocket-lab-acquire-iridium-historic-deal-creating-fully)).

**A New York Times report that OpenAI may push its IPO to 2027 rattled the AI trade and dragged semiconductors lower.** The company, which confidentially filed on June 8, is reportedly weighing whether to debut this year below a $1 trillion valuation or wait for a cleaner shot at the trillion-dollar mark, with CFO Sarah Friar said to favor delay to firm up public-company reporting. Advisors have grown cautious after SpaceX's public debut rallied and then faded, raising doubts about retail appetite. The report triggered a reassessment of near-term AI chip demand; SoftBank, which holds a roughly $65 billion stake, saw shares fall sharply ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/26/openai-ipo-timeline-delayed-kalshi-predictions.html), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/article/ai-trade-hits-a-wall-amid-report-that-openai-will-delay-ipo-until-2027-150642366.html)).

**Qualcomm is buying AI software startup Modular for about $3.92 billion in an all-stock deal to deepen its data-center ambitions.** Modular, founded in 2022 by Chris Lattner and Tim Davis, builds an AI-native software platform that lets models run efficiently across different accelerators without rewriting code — a direct play at Nvidia's CUDA moat and at the AI inference market. Qualcomm expects to issue up to 19.2 million shares and fold in Modular's roughly 150 employees, with the deal closing in the second half of 2026 pending antitrust clearance. The price marks a steep premium: Modular raised $250 million at a $1.6 billion valuation just nine months ago ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/qualcomm-ai-chip-modular-software.html), [Qualcomm](https://investor.qualcomm.com/news-events/press-releases/news-details/2026/Qualcomm-to-Acquire-Modular/default.aspx)).

**Micron's blowout results underscored the widening gap between AI fundamentals and jittery chip-stock sentiment.** Fiscal Q3 revenue more than quadrupled to $41.46 billion, topping the $35.84 billion consensus, and the company guided current-quarter revenue to about $50 billion, up from $11.3 billion a year earlier, on soaring memory demand tied to AI. Micron said it has signed 16 long-term customer agreements and expects roughly half its revenue to fall under those contracts. Yet the stock swung violently in the days after — up 15% on the print, then tumbling amid a broader semiconductor selloff — as investors weighed AI infrastructure costs against the OpenAI IPO headlines ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/micron-mu-earnings-report-q3-2026.html), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/06/25/why-did-stock-market-tech-selloff-stop-micron-technology/)).

## Enterprise Software

**Gartner warned that up to $234 billion of enterprise application spending is exposed to "agentic arbitrage" through 2030 — roughly 20% of enterprise SaaS.** The firm argues that as AI agents complete tasks across multiple systems, demand for traditional seat-based software interfaces erodes, pressuring incumbents to re-price and re-architect around agents ([Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-07-01-gartner-says-us-dollars-234-billion-in-enterprise-application-software-spend-is-at-risk-from-agentic-artificial-intelligence)). The theme is already visible in the field: Levi Strauss has built specialized agents across HR, finance, IT, and retail and is now stitching them into a single "Super Agent," while Ramp's Applied AI Solutions targets multi-system workflows that require judgment on exceptions ([PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/news/artificial-intelligence/2026/super-agents-are-connecting-what-enterprise-software-kept-separate)).

**Anthropic's Claude models reached general availability inside Microsoft Foundry, running on Nvidia GB300 Blackwell Ultra systems.** Azure customers can now access Claude — including the newly GA Claude Sonnet 5 — through existing subscriptions with the same identity, governance, and billing they use for Microsoft's own AI services, choosing between Azure-hosted and Anthropic-hosted deployments ([Microsoft Azure](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/blog/introducing-anthropics-claude-models-in-microsoft-foundry-bringing-frontier-intelligence-to-azure/)). Separately, Claude Opus 4.8 landed on Google's Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform, giving buyers cross-cloud optionality for complex workflows. Elsewhere in the stack, IBM and ServiceNow expanded a collaboration to scale enterprise AI, ServiceNow and Accenture launched a forward-deployed engineering program, and ServiceNow set July 22 for Q2 results ([Business Wire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260701382890/en/ServiceNow-to-Announce-Second-Quarter-2026-Financial-Results-on-July-22)). Microsoft's commercial Microsoft 365 price increases also took effect July 1, which the company attributes to added AI, security, and management capabilities.

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**Together AI raised an $800 million Series C at an $8.3 billion post-money valuation, more than doubling its worth in five months.** The round — led by Aramco Ventures with Vista Equity, General Catalyst, Nvidia, Emergence, March Capital, and others — funds the open-model inference and training platform's plan to grow compute capacity roughly 50-fold over five years. Together said annual bookings crossed $1.15 billion last quarter as open-source model usage tripled, with customers including Cursor, Cognition, and Decagon ([Business Wire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260701243402/en/Together-AI-Raises-$800-Million-at-$8.3-Billion-Valuation-to-Make-Frontier-AI-Accessible-to-All), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/together-ai-raises-800-million-180132872.html)).

**Capital kept concentrating in the AI stack and deep tech.** Ottawa-based Dominion Dynamics raised $100 million in what was billed as Canada's largest defense-tech Series A, for its AuraNet command-and-control platform and Scout robotic wingman. Chip-design startup Oxmiq raised $35 million to fuse GPU, CPU, and TPU into one architecture, and Omen AI took $31 million to monitor data-center coolant health. Blockchain-data platform Allium closed a $40 million Series B, small-business growth platform Pie raised a $19.5 million Series A, and autonomous-pharmacy startup Queue emerged from stealth with a $12.6 million seed ([Tech Startups](https://techstartups.com/2026/07/01/venture-capital-startup-funding-roundup-july-1-2026/)). The through-line, per founders and investors: money is flowing to AI infrastructure, fintech rails, defense, and robotics, while generic software gets passed over.

## Tech Investing & Markets

**Markets opened the second half on shaky footing as profit-taking hit the names that led the first-half rally.** The Nasdaq had climbed 1.5% to 26,213.72 on July 1, but by July 2 the S&P 500 slipped about 0.22% to 7,483.23 and the Nasdaq Composite fell 0.66% as investors dumped semiconductors that had surged more than 80% in the first half ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-july-1-2026-nasdaq-futures-slip-after-strongest-quarter-since-2020)). Sandisk shed more than 10% even while sitting up over 750% year to date, a reminder of how stretched positioning had become in memory and storage.

**IPO activity showed the window is open but selective.** Italy's Bending Spoons — operator of acquired brands including AOL and Vimeo — went public on Nasdaq, raising $1.7 billion at a reported $18.4 billion valuation. Blank-check vehicle Viking Acquisition Corp. II priced a $200 million IPO on the NYSE. Bankers continue to eye Databricks (last marked around $134 billion) and Canva (about $42 billion) as marquee 2026 candidates, though the OpenAI delay is a cautionary signal for the largest debuts ([Forge](https://forgeglobal.com/tech-ipo-calendar-2026/), [PwC](https://www.pwc.com/us/en/industries/tmt/library/technology-deals-outlook.html)).

## AI & Emerging Tech

**Autonomy and robotics kept moving from demo to deployment.** Uber, WeRide, and AVOMO launched Spain's first commercial robotaxi service in the Madrid region, an early European foothold for autonomous ride-hailing, while BYD confirmed its entry into humanoid robotics — extending the EV maker's manufacturing muscle into embodied AI ([artificialintelligence-news.com](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/)). On the model front, adoption metrics continue to compound: organizational AI use has reached roughly 88%, ChatGPT crossed one billion monthly active users, and coding benchmarks like SWE-bench Verified jumped from about 60% to near-100% in a year, per Stanford's 2026 AI Index ([Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report)). Adobe also expanded its creative-AI stack with an agreement to acquire Topaz Labs. The consistent message across these threads: the durable advantage is in workflow design and data discipline, not raw model access.

## Hot Takes & Commentary

The OpenAI IPO wobble is a useful stress test for the AI narrative. Fundamentals — Micron's quadrupling revenue, Together AI's $1.15 billion bookings run-rate — are unambiguously strong, yet chip stocks sold off on a scheduling rumor. That gap says positioning, not demand, is the near-term risk, and it explains why the highest-quality names are swinging hardest. Meanwhile, Gartner's $234 billion "agentic arbitrage" call and Qualcomm's Modular buy point at the same structural shift: value is migrating from software seats and single-vendor accelerators toward agents and portable inference. The companies buying their way into that layer — Qualcomm, Rocket Lab going vertical in space — are betting that owning the full stack beats renting a slice of it.
