## Top Stories

**TSMC posted record Q2 results—net income up 77% to NT$706.6B ($4.31/ADR)—then promptly triggered a global tech selloff.** Revenue rose 36% year-over-year to NT$1.27 trillion, gross margin hit 67.7%, and the company raised its full-year revenue growth outlook from 30%-plus to above 40%, citing surging demand for AI accelerators and agentic AI workloads. But TSMC also hiked its 2026 capex budget from ~$56B to $60–64B and announced an additional [$100 billion investment in Arizona](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/tsmc-q2-2026-earnings-record-112109987.html), bringing total committed U.S. spending to $265B. Investors fixated on the spending surge: TSMC shares fell 7.3% in Taipei, the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) dropped nearly 4%, and [Arm Holdings slid more than 5%](https://fortune.com/2026/07/17/tech-stocks-global-selloff-as-investors-ai-semiconductor-chips/). Nasdaq-100 futures were off 1.9% before Friday's open. Apollo warned a hyperscaler pullback could tip the economy into recession.

**Google is expected to launch Gemini 3.5 Pro today, July 17, after scrapping and rebuilding the original base model.** The release reportedly features a [2-million-token context window](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320308/20260713/gemini-35-pro-targets-july-17-after-full-rebuild-every-spec-remains-unconfirmed.htm)—double Gemini 2.5 Pro—plus a "Deep Think" reasoning mode and stronger agentic coding capabilities. Google DeepMind restarted pretraining after finding structural failures in recursive tool-calling and SVG generation. The launch lands in a crowded week: OpenAI shipped [GPT-5.6 Sol](https://the-decoder.com/anthropic-extends-free-fable-5-access-for-subscribers-as-openais-gpt-5-6-sol-heats-up-the-pricing-war/) on July 9 and SpaceXAI released [Grok 4.5](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-releases-grok-4-5-which-elon-describes-as-an-opus-class-model/) the same day, creating the most compressed frontier-model release cycle to date.

**President Xi Jinping delivered the keynote at the World Artificial Intelligence Conference in Shanghai—his first appearance at the event since its 2018 inception.** Xi declared AI development ["should not be a solo performance by any single country but rather a symphony of global cooperation"](https://www.kpbs.org/news/international/2026/07/17/chinas-xi-calls-for-step-up-of-global-effort-in-ai-as-us-curbs-squeeze-chinas-tech-access) while objecting to the "overstretching" of national security restrictions. A day earlier, 29 countries signed an agreement to [establish a World AI Cooperation Organization](https://thenextweb.com/news/xi-jinping-waic-2026-shanghai-ai-speech) headquartered in Shanghai. The four-day conference features 1,400 guests, 1,100 exhibitors, and over 300 global product debuts.

**SpaceX's Starship Flight 13 aborted seconds before liftoff after four of 33 Raptor engines failed to ignite.** The [launch abort](https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/07/16/live-coverage-spacex-to-deploy-first-starlink-v3-satellites-on-suborbital-starship-super-heavy-flight/) at Starbase in southern Texas was the first full-scale pad abort in the program's history. Twenty next-gen Starlink v3 satellites were aboard. Elon Musk said two engines will be replaced, with a retry expected early next week.

## Enterprise Software

**Gartner warned that $234 billion in enterprise application software spending is exposed to "agentic arbitrage" by 2030.** The research firm said AI agents completing tasks across multiple systems will [break the link between user growth and revenue growth](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-07-01-gartner-says-us-dollars-234-billion-in-enterprise-application-software-spend-is-at-risk-from-agentic-artificial-intelligence), effectively making traditional UX-heavy applications invisible. Gartner described the shift as a "SaaSpocalypse"—the disaggregation of legacy SaaS markets as AI-native startups deliver measurable outcomes instead of features.

**NetApp acquired DataPelago, a California-based AI data infrastructure company, to bring GPU-accelerated processing to the storage layer.** DataPelago's Nucleus engine processes data using CPUs and GPUs without moving it to external compute clusters, claiming [up to 80% cost reduction and 10x performance gains](https://www.netapp.com/newsroom/press-releases/news-rel-20260716-210092/). DataPelago will operate as a wholly owned subsidiary. Financial terms were not disclosed.

**Microsoft launched Frontier Company, a $2.5 billion AI consulting organization deploying 6,000 industry and engineering experts to help enterprises plan and deploy AI.** Separately, [cybersecurity M&A hit a record 219 deals totaling $9.1B in H1 2026](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/articles/momentum-cyber-mid-2026-review-175600569.html), with AI security acquisitions nearly tripling year-over-year from 10 to 29. Notable deals included Accenture's $4.175B acquisition of Dragos, NetRise, and runZero, and 1Password's $250–300M purchase of Apono.

JPMorganChase selected SambaNova as an inference-infrastructure partner for secure on-premises AI.

Together AI reported annual bookings exceeding $1.15 billion and secured commitments for over 500 MW of compute capacity.

## Tech Investing & Markets

**The global chip selloff dominated Friday's pre-market action.** Despite TSMC's blowout earnings, investors sold on the capex hike. [Taiwan stocks fell into a technical correction](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/chip-stock-selloff-deepens-in-asia-as-tsmc-fails-to-impress), SoftBank sank over 9% in Tokyo, and SK Hynix posted a record selloff. The iShares Semiconductor ETF slipped 3.7% pre-market. The S&P 500 had already lost 0.51% Thursday and the Nasdaq Composite declined 1.47%. Analysts are increasingly questioning whether AI infrastructure spending can sustain current growth rates.

**The AI model pricing war accelerated across three fronts.** Anthropic extended free [Fable 5 access through July 19](https://www.forbes.com/sites/tylerroush/2026/07/13/ai-model-wars-anthropic-extends-fable-access-again-after-openais-sol-release/) for the third time in five weeks, responding to OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol launching at half the input-token price ($5 vs $10 per million). SpaceXAI's Grok 4.5 undercut both at [$2 input / $6 output per million tokens](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-releases-grok-4-5-which-elon-describes-as-an-opus-class-model/), positioning as an "Opus-class model" at a fraction of the cost. Chinese AI providers now serve approximately 45% of all OpenRouter traffic, up from under 2% a year ago.

**OpenAI proposed giving the U.S. government a 5% equity stake worth ~$42.6 billion at its $852 billion valuation.** CEO Sam Altman framed the [donated equity](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/02/openai-proposes-us-government-own-5percent-stake-to-address-political-blowback.html) as a way to share AI's upside with taxpayers, modeled on Alaska's Permanent Fund. The structure could extend to Anthropic, Google, and Meta, though none have confirmed involvement. Talks with Treasury and Commerce officials remain in early stages and would require Congressional approval.

## AI & Emerging Tech

**Apple Intelligence received regulatory clearance to launch in China, powered by Alibaba's Qwen models rather than Apple's own.** China's Cyberspace Administration [registered Apple's on-device AI service](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/07/15/apple-intelligence-cleared-to-launch-in-china/) on July 15, with Baidu providing additional model support. No launch date was announced, but approval typically precedes rollout by a few months. Alibaba's U.S.-listed shares rose 3.7% on the news.

**Over 200 economists and AI researchers—including 16 Nobel laureates—signed a Stanford-organized open letter demanding policymakers ["act now" to prepare for AI's economic impact](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/13/hundreds-of-experts-warn-the-world-must-prepare-now-for-ais-impact).** The letter warns that existing policy frameworks are inadequate for the speed and scale of labor displacement AI may cause.

**PrismML's Bonsai 27B, released July 14, compressed a 27-billion-parameter model to 3.9 gigabytes and runs on an iPhone 17 Pro at ~11 tokens per second.** The 1-bit quantized version retains over 90% of full-precision performance, advancing on-device AI capabilities significantly.

## Hot Takes & Commentary

**Gartner's "SaaSpocalypse" framing deserves attention beyond the headline.** The core insight—that agentic AI breaks the user-seat licensing model by making software invisible—has immediate implications for how investors value SaaS multiples. If agents complete workflows across systems without human interaction, per-seat pricing becomes indefensible. Early movers rewriting their pricing around outcomes rather than access will capture the transition; incumbents clinging to seat-based models face margin compression well before 2030.

**The TSMC capex paradox is the market's real anxiety.** Record demand, record margins, record investment—and the stock falls 7%. Investors aren't questioning AI demand; they're questioning whether the infrastructure buildout can sustain returns at this scale, especially as Chinese and open-source alternatives erode pricing power downstream. The selloff may prove to be a buying opportunity, but the market is clearly signaling that "spend more to earn more" has limits.
