## Top Stories

**Nvidia delivers another blockbuster quarter, announces massive buyback.** Nvidia reported fiscal Q1 revenue of [$81.6 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/the-tech-download-what-you-might-have-missed-in-nvidias-earnings.html), up 85% year-over-year, driven by record data center revenue of $75.2 billion. The company unveiled an $80 billion share repurchase authorization and hiked its quarterly dividend to $0.25 per share from $0.01. CFO Colette Kress highlighted that Nvidia's new Vera CPU opens a [$200 billion addressable market](https://www.kiplinger.com/investing/live/nvidia-earnings-live-updates-and-commentary-may-2026), with $20 billion in CPU revenue expected this year. CEO Jensen Huang acknowledged the company has "largely conceded" China's AI chip market to Huawei. Shares dipped roughly 2% on Thursday despite the beat.

**SpaceX files record $80B IPO prospectus, pricing set for June 11.** SpaceX's S-1 [revealed](https://fortune.com/2026/05/20/spacex-finally-files-ipo-prospectus-reveals-revenue-is-up-but-losses-are-too/) consolidated 2025 revenue of $18.7 billion with $6.6 billion in adjusted EBITDA but a $2.6 billion operating loss. Starlink accounted for over two-thirds of revenue and [earned $1.2 billion in profit](https://satnews.com/2026/05/21/inside-spacexs-s-1-three-companies-one-profit-1-75-trillion/) last quarter. The filing disclosed Elon Musk received 1 billion performance-based Class B shares in January, with one vesting condition requiring a permanent Mars colony of one million inhabitants. Goldman Sachs is lead underwriter, with trading expected to begin June 12 under ticker SPCX at a roughly [$1.75 trillion valuation](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/spacex-ipo-live-updates.html).

**OpenAI preparing confidential IPO filing as soon as this week.** OpenAI is working with Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley on a [draft prospectus](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/openai-ipo-filing.html), targeting a public listing by September at a valuation exceeding $1 trillion. The company is currently valued at more than $850 billion by private investors. The resolution of Elon Musk's lawsuit removed a major legal overhang and appears to have accelerated the timeline.

**Anthropic projects first-ever operating profit on $10.9B Q2 revenue.** Anthropic told investors it expects Q2 revenue of [$10.9 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/anthropic-revenue-explosive-growth-ipo-profitable-quarter.html), up 130% from $4.8 billion in Q1, and roughly $559 million in operating profit — its first. The company cautioned that profitability may not hold through the year as compute costs escalate. Separately, Anthropic is in [early talks with Microsoft](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/anthropic-microsoft-maia-200-ai-chip.html) to run Claude inference on Microsoft's custom Maia 200 AI chips, diversifying beyond its existing $100 billion AWS Trainium arrangement.

**U.S. awards $2 billion in quantum computing grants, takes equity stakes.** The Department of Commerce signed letters of intent to provide [$2 billion](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/05/department-commerce-announces-letters-intent-9-companies-2-billion) under the CHIPS Act to nine quantum firms. IBM leads with $1 billion toward a new quantum wafer foundry called Anderon. GlobalFoundries will receive $375 million, while D-Wave, Rigetti, and Infleqtion each land about $100 million. The government will take [minority equity stakes](https://fortune.com/2026/05/22/us-award-2-billion-grants-nine-quantum-computing-companies-equity-stakes/) in each company. Quantum stocks surged as much as 30% on the news.

## Enterprise Software

**Workday raises full-year margin guidance on AI strength.** Workday posted Q1 fiscal 2027 subscription revenue of [$2.455 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/workday-wday-q1-earnings-report-2027.html), up 13% year-over-year, and raised its non-GAAP operating margin guidance to 30.5%, incorporating accelerated AI investment across product and go-to-market. Shares jumped as much as 14% in after-hours trading.

**Zscaler acquires Symmetry Systems for AI agent security.** Zscaler announced the [acquisition](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/21/zscaler-acquires-symmetry-systems-extend-ai-agent-security-capabilities/) of Symmetry Systems, whose access graph technology maps how human and non-human identities interact across enterprises. The deal extends Zscaler's zero trust platform to govern how AI agents communicate with applications and data. Terms were not disclosed, and the transaction is expected to close in days.

**Kore.ai launches Artemis platform, takes on Salesforce and ServiceNow.** Kore.ai [unveiled Artemis](https://venturebeat.com/technology/kore-ai-launches-artemis-ai-agent-platform-expands-challenge-to-microsoft-and-salesforce/), an AI agent platform designed for enterprise service management, expanding the company's challenge to incumbent ITSM and CRM vendors.

**Dell Technologies World spotlights open-source AI and enterprise sovereignty.** Dell's annual conference is underway with sessions highlighting how [open-source AI agents](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/22/open-source-ai-agents-drive-dells-agentic-moment-delltechworld/) are driving enterprise adoption, while [IBM launched Sovereign Core](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/22/managing-digital-sovereignty-enterprise-ibmthink/) at its Think conference to address data sovereignty requirements for AI workloads.

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**Hark raises $700M Series A at $6B valuation for "personalized intelligence" devices.** Brett Adcock's AI hardware startup [closed a $700 million Series A](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/21/hark-raises-700m-series-a-for-its-secretive-universal-ai-interface/) led by Parkway Venture Capital, with Nvidia, Intel Capital, AMD Ventures, Qualcomm Ventures, and Salesforce Ventures participating. Hark plans to debut software-based AI models this summer, followed by dedicated hardware. Adcock previously founded Figure AI and Archer Aviation.

**Modal Labs seals $355M round, quadruples valuation to $4.65B.** The serverless AI infrastructure startup [raised $355 million](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/21/serverless-ai-infrastructure-startup-modal-labs-seals-355m-funding-round/) led by Redpoint Ventures and General Catalyst, up from a $1.1 billion valuation in September. Modal helps developers deploy AI inference workloads without managing cloud servers directly.

**Mercury lands $200M at $5.2B valuation.** The digital banking startup [closed a Series D](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/fintech-funding-digital-banking-startup-mercury-lands-200m/) led by TCV, with participation from a16z, Coatue, Sequoia, and others. Mercury serves over 200,000 startups and businesses.

**Convective Capital raises $85M for disaster resilience.** After launching to invest in fire tech, Convective is [broadening its mandate](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/21/convective-capital-raises-an-85-million-fund-to-build-disaster-resilience/) to cover a wider range of climate-related disaster resilience technologies.

## Tech Investing & Markets

**Lenovo posts record year, AI revenue doubles.** Lenovo reported [full-year revenue of $83.1 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/lenovo-shares-jump-15percent-on-record-earnings-as-ai-revenue-nearly-doubles.html) with AI-related sales growing 105% for the year. Q4 revenue hit $21.6 billion, up 27%, with AI accounting for 38% of quarterly revenue. Shares surged nearly 20% in Hong Kong. Lenovo is targeting $100 billion in revenue within two years.

**AMD commits $10B+ to Taiwan AI chip ecosystem.** AMD [announced](https://ir.amd.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1286/amd-announces-more-than-10-billion-in-taiwan-ecosystem-investments-to-accelerate-ai-infrastructure) more than $10 billion in Taiwan investments focused on advanced packaging manufacturing and next-generation interconnect technology for its Helios AI platform, with multi-gigawatt deployments planned for the second half of 2026.

**Mega-IPO wave raises market-top concerns.** Analysts are [flagging the cluster](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/ipo-flurry-top-market-analysts-ai-spacex-musk-altman.html) of SpaceX, OpenAI, and other mega-IPOs as a potential signal of late-cycle exuberance. SpaceX's June 12 offering would be the largest float in history.

**Oura files confidentially for IPO.** The Finnish smart ring maker, valued at [$11 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/oura-smart-ring-ipo-filing.html) after a $900 million Series E, has filed a draft prospectus with the SEC. Oura is on track to surpass five million paid members and could generate close to $2 billion in 2026 revenue.

**Spotify surges 16% on Universal Music AI deal.** Spotify and UMG [struck a landmark licensing agreement](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/21/spotify-and-universal-music-strike-deal-allowing-fan-made-ai-covers-and-remixes/) allowing Premium subscribers to create AI-generated covers and remixes of participating artists' catalogues. The opt-in framework is built on consent, credit, and compensation.

## AI & Emerging Tech

**Trump postpones AI executive order.** The White House [pulled](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/trump-ai-executive-order-postponed.html) a planned executive order that would have established voluntary pre-release government review of advanced AI models. Trump cited concerns it "could have been a blocker" to U.S. AI competitiveness.

**Alibaba's Qwen3.7-Max can run 35 hours autonomously.** Alibaba's proprietary model [now supports](https://venturebeat.com/technology/alibabas-proprietary-qwen3-7-max-can-run-for-35-hours-autonomously-and-supports-external-harnesses-like-anthropics-claude-code) external harnesses including Anthropic's Claude Code, marking a step toward truly long-running AI agent workflows.

**Meta, Broadcom launch $125M semiconductor research hub at UCLA.** Five companies — Broadcom, Meta, Applied Materials, GlobalFoundries, and Synopsys — are [funding a five-year Semiconductor Hub](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/meta-broadcom-others-to-launch-125-million-semiconductor-research-hub-at-ucla.html) focused on AI-native hardware, advanced packaging, and next-generation computing systems.

**Meta quietly launches Forum, a Reddit-style app built on Facebook Groups.** The new iOS app features AI-powered Q&A across groups and [admin AI assistants](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/22/meta-quietly-launches-a-new-reddit-like-app-called-forum/) for moderation. No official announcement was made.

## Hot Takes & Commentary

The IPO pipeline is raising eyebrows. SpaceX at $1.75 trillion and OpenAI targeting $1 trillion-plus would represent the two largest tech debuts in history, landing within months of each other. As [CNBC analysts noted](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/ipo-flurry-top-market-analysts-ai-spacex-musk-altman.html), mega-IPOs clustering together have historically preceded corrections.

Meanwhile, venture capital concentration continues accelerating. Single rounds north of $300 million — Hark at $700M, Modal Labs at $355M — are becoming routine in AI infrastructure. As [Crunchbase reported](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/data-capital-concentrating-faster-startups-100m-ai/), capital is concentrating faster than ever, raising questions about what happens to everyone else.

GitHub's stumble is worth watching. CNBC [reported](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/microsoft-was-positioned-to-win-in-ai-coding-outages-got-in-the-way.html) that Microsoft's GitHub has fallen behind newer rivals in vibe coding despite its massive user base, with reliability issues opening the door for competitors.
