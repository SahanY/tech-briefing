## Top Stories

**President Trump announced that Apple has agreed to design and build chips with Intel, sending Intel shares up 10% to $133.82 on Thursday.** The partnership, building on a preliminary agreement reached in May, would allow Apple to diversify its supply chain beyond TSMC and gives Intel a marquee customer for its 18A-P process node. Neither company has officially confirmed the deal — Intel declined to comment on "a potential Apple–Intel agreement" and Apple did not respond to requests. The move aligns with the administration's push to reshore semiconductor manufacturing amid persistent geopolitical tensions over Taiwan. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/trump-intel-apple-chip-design-deal.html), [The Hill](https://thehill.com/homenews/5929739-donald-trump-apple-intel-us-chip-production/))

**The Commerce Department's unprecedented export control action against Anthropic is drawing legal scrutiny and industry alarm.** On June 12, Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick ordered Anthropic to disable its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models globally after another company claimed to have jailbroken Mythos. Because Anthropic cannot reliably distinguish foreign nationals from U.S. persons across hundreds of millions of users, the practical result was a hard shutoff for all customers worldwide. Anthropic called the action a "misunderstanding" and noted access to Claude models, including Opus 4.8, was unaffected. The CSIS and legal analysts are questioning whether BIS has the authority to dictate access to AI systems under export control law. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-19/lutnick-s-anthropic-crackdown-claims-new-power-over-ai-models), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/12/anthropic-trump-mythos-fable-national-security), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/06/13/anthropic-disables-fable-mythos-export-controls-national-security-threat/))

**The U.S. told ASML it believes one of the company's top EUV lithography machines may have reached China, violating long-standing export restrictions.** Commerce Secretary Lutnick raised the concern directly with ASML executives in recent meetings. ASML responded Friday that it has never shipped an EUV system to China and there is commercial logic cutting against such a transfer. The dispute escalates Washington's campaign to restrict China's access to advanced chipmaking equipment, with legislation proposed in April that would require allies to align with U.S. export controls. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/19/the-us-says-asmls-top-chip-tool-may-be-in-china-asml-says-it-isnt/), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-19/us-tells-asml-it-s-concerned-china-may-have-top-chip-tool))

**Apple CEO Tim Cook warned that device price increases are "unavoidable" as a global memory chip shortage driven by AI data center demand squeezes consumer electronics makers.** Cook compared the crisis to a "hundred-year flood," with the iPhone 18 lineup expected to absorb meaningful price hikes later this year. Estimates suggest the iPhone Pro 19 could cost upward of $1,299. The shortage is forcing consumer tech companies into direct competition with hyperscalers for dwindling DRAM and NAND supplies. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/19/memory-crisis-hits-such-extremes-that-even-apple-cant-be-safe-.html), [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/17/apple-confirms-price-increases-are-coming-to-its-products-due-to-ram-shortage/))

## Enterprise Software

**Databricks launched Genie One at its Data + AI Summit, an agentic coworker that integrates with third-party tools including Google Drive, Jira, Slack, and SharePoint.** Unlike earlier Genie products focused on Databricks-internal analytics, Genie One creates documents, runs scheduled tasks, and spins up agents from a conversation. Available on web, iOS, Android, Slack, and Teams, the product is Databricks' clearest signal yet that it intends to be an enterprise AI platform, not just a data lakehouse. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/16/databricks-new-agentic-coworker-genie-one-brings-ai-automation-every-part-business/), [Databricks](https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/databricks-launches-genie-one-all-new-agentic-coworker-every-team))

**Elastic agreed to acquire CRV-backed DeductiveAI for up to $85 million, adding AI-powered bug detection and site reliability engineering automation.** DeductiveAI was founded in 2023 and came out of stealth just seven months ago with a $7.5 million seed at a $33 million valuation — making this a quick and lucrative exit in the fast-growing AI SRE sector. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/source-elastic-agrees-to-buy-crv-backed-deductiveai-for-up-to-85m/))

**The EU is preparing to release preliminary findings as early as next week classifying AWS and Azure as "gatekeepers" under the Digital Markets Act.** The designation would compel both hyperscalers to ease data portability and abandon restrictive licensing and egress fees. The Commission aims to conclude its market investigation by November 2026. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-18/microsoft-amazon-cloud-services-face-tough-eu-antitrust-law))

([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/ai-inference-startup-baseten-reportedly-raising-1-5b-months-after-its-last-mega-round/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/ai-inference-provider-baseten-reportedly-raising-1-5b-funding/))

([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/dream-raises-260m-sovereign-ai-cybersecurity-tools/), [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/dream-raises-260-million-at-3-billion-valuation/))

([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/agentic-marketing-ai-startup-gradial-grabs-65m-fresh-funding/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/architect-labs-nabs-24m-speed-chip-design-projects-ai/))
