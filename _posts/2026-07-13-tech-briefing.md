## Top Stories

**Apple sued OpenAI in federal court, alleging a coordinated scheme to steal trade secrets for OpenAI's upcoming consumer hardware.** The complaint, filed Friday in the Northern District of California, accuses OpenAI of poaching Apple engineers to extract confidential designs, manufacturing processes, and supply-chain strategy. Apple names hardware chief and former Apple VP Tang Tan, who it says directed candidates still employed at Apple to bring "actual parts" to interviews for "show and tell," and Chang Liu, who allegedly downloaded dozens of confidential hardware files before leaving in January. It is a striking reversal for two firms that integrated ChatGPT into iOS in 2024, and the suit threatens to complicate OpenAI's push to build a device that rivals the iPhone ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html); [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-over-alleged-trade-secret-theft/); [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/how-apple-s-lawsuit-threatens-to-disrupt-openai-s-bid-to-rival-the-iphone)). The feud spilled onto X over the weekend, where Elon Musk and Sam Altman traded barbs after the filing ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html)).

**TSMC reported June revenue up nearly 68% year-over-year to T$442.7 billion, capping first-half growth of about 36% and underscoring that AI chip demand remains unrelenting.** The world's largest contract chipmaker is sold out on its N3 node — the process targeted by nearly every leading AI GPU and CPU this year — with Nvidia reportedly reserving roughly 60% of advanced packaging capacity for 2026. Analysts now peg TSMC on track for more than $40 billion in AI chip revenue this year, close to a quarter of total sales. The company has guided to full-year revenue growth above 30% and capex of $52–56 billion ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/tsmc-june-revenue-rises-percent-ahead-second-quarter.html); [Digitimes](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20260713VL213/tsmc-revenue-growth-demand-2026.html)) ahead of its Thursday earnings report.

**Meta committed an additional $40 billion to its Richland Parish, Louisiana data center, pushing total expected investment past $250 billion and expanding the site to at least 5 gigawatts.** The near-doubling — the headline commitment now exceeds $50 billion, aided by generous state tax incentives — makes it Meta's largest data center and one of the most expensive private infrastructure projects in the country. It caps a run of hyperscaler buildout announcements even as Wall Street questions AI returns ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/meta-louisiana-data-center-investment-reaches-50-billion-amid-ai-push.html); [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/meta-s-louisiana-data-center-to-surpass-250-billion-price-tag)).

**Munich-based defense startup Helsing raised $1.8 billion in a Series E at an $18 billion valuation, Europe's biggest-ever defense-tech round.** Demand "significantly" exceeded allocation, drawing JPMorgan, Lightspeed, Iconiq, Dragoneer, General Catalyst, Goldman Sachs, and Canada's CPP Investments. Founded only in 2021, Helsing builds drones and autonomous software — its HX-2 loitering munitions are being supplied to Ukraine — and now ranks among Europe's most valuable defense companies ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/helsing-fund-raise-defense-18-billion.html); [Defense News](https://www.defensenews.com/global/europe/2026/07/13/helsing-raises-18-billion-in-europes-biggest-defense-startup-round/)).

## Enterprise Software

**OpenAI launched ChatGPT Work, an autonomous agent that gathers context across a user's connected apps, decomposes a goal into steps, and returns finished spreadsheets, slides, docs, and web apps.** Announced July 9 and powered by GPT-5.6, the agent is designed to stay with complex projects "for hours" and follows Codex-style token-based credit pricing for enterprise and edu workspaces. The launch coincides with the Codex app folding into a single ChatGPT desktop app and OpenAI beginning to sunset its Atlas browser — a clear signal that OpenAI intends to compete directly in the enterprise productivity stack ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/openai-unveils-chatgpt-work-agent-to-field-tasks-for-hours); [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/news/artificial-intelligence/2026/openai-launches-chatgpt-agent-that-executes-complex-workflows/)).

**Cloudflare pressed its bid to become the toll booth for machine traffic, launching Precursor to catch bots by analyzing entire sessions and finalizing default rules that block AI training and agent crawlers on ad-supported pages starting September 15.** CEO Matthew Prince has framed the internet as increasingly used by agents and machines rather than humans; the company also rolled out BotBase, a searchable directory of known bots, and content-compensation partnerships with You.com and Ceramic.ai ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/13/cloudflare-launches-precursor-to-catch-bots-by-watching-entire-sessions/); [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/01/cloudflares-new-policy-pushes-ai-companies-to-pay-for-publishers-content/)). VentureBeat's reporting captured the counter-current in enterprise AI adoption: 86% of enterprises say their GPUs run at half capacity or less, and a majority report having watched AI agents be "confidently wrong," fueling demand for an evaluation and context layer before agents gain more autonomy ([VentureBeat](https://feeds.feedburner.com/venturebeat/SZYF)).

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**Capital continued to concentrate in AI, defense, and energy infrastructure.** SambaNova closed a first tranche of a $1 billion Series F at an $11 billion valuation (General Atlantic), Together AI raised $800 million at $8.3 billion, and defense-and-autonomy firm Quantum Systems pulled in $1.2 billion (Blackstone, Airbus, Fidelity). Salesforce led a $135 million Series A for agentic software builder 8090 Solutions, Khosla backed legal-AI platform Norm AI with $120 million, and Prime Intellect raised $130 million with NVIDIA and Intel Capital participating ([Crunchbase](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/biggest-funding-rounds-ai-energy-biotech-joulent/)). China's fundraising rush intensified: open-source model developer MiniMax secured roughly $2 billion via a Hong Kong share sale and convertible bonds, with CEO Yan Junjie vowing to forgo salary until AGI and to give away 5% of his stake; the company is building a 2.5–2.7 trillion-parameter M3 Pro model it plans to open-source in Q3 ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/10/open-source-ai-model-developer-minimax-raises-2b-funding/)). Data-scraping infrastructure startup Oxylabs raised $130 million in its first outside round ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/feed)). Zooming out, Crunchbase reported North American startup funding shattered records in the first half of 2026, driven by AI, while Europe posted its strongest venture quarter in four years ([Crunchbase](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/europe-strongest-venture-quarter-uk-ma-eq-2q-2026/)).

## Tech Investing & Markets

**A rotation out of AI infrastructure and into Apple defined the tape.** Investors nervous about AI capex returns pushed chip and cloud names lower while piling into Apple, whose shares have rallied roughly 15% off their June 25 bottom — adding close to $600 billion in market value and returning the stock to record territory after a poorly received AI features presentation ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/apple-s-600-billion-rally-fueled-by-traders-fleeing-ai-selloff)). The broader message from Wall Street has shifted from rewarding AI spending to demanding evidence it will generate outsized returns, with added anxiety over the possibility of a Fed rate increase later this year as oil-driven inflation persists ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/26/global-tech-stocks-ai-infrastructure-costs-selloff-softbank-apple.html)). Chip supply chains stayed in focus: Intel said it will invest €5 billion to expand its Irish hub in the AI chip race, and Goldman Sachs flagged two stocks positioned to benefit from a chip-designer shortage ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/intel-invests-5-billion-to-expand-irish-hub-in-ai-chip-race); [CNBC](https://search.cnbc.com/rs/search/combinedcms/view.xml?partnerId=wrss01&id=19854910)). SK Hynix shares sold off, though Bloomberg noted analysts see it as a manageable pullback rather than a structural memory-demand problem ([Bloomberg](https://feeds.bloomberg.com/technology/news.rss)).

## AI & Emerging Tech

**Regulation and infrastructure dominated the AI headlines beyond the fundraising.** The European Commission issued preliminary findings that Meta's "addictive" design features — infinite scroll, autoplay, push notifications, personalized recommendations — breach the Digital Services Act, exposing the company to fines up to 6% of global revenue, or nearly $12 billion; Meta said it disagrees and will respond ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/eu-threatens-meta-with-fines-over-addictive-features-on-facebook-and-instagram/); [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/meta-instagram-facebook-addictive-design-breach-eu-laws.html)). Washington is separately weighing measures to keep China from training its models on US AI systems ([Bloomberg](https://feeds.bloomberg.com/technology/news.rss)). On the frontier-hardware front, Elon Musk's Neuralink continued human implant trials while China's BrainCo pursued a wearable brain-tech approach ([CNBC](https://search.cnbc.com/rs/search/combinedcms/view.xml?partnerId=wrss01&id=19854910)). And SpaceX cleared FAA review of its May Super Heavy booster failure, opening the door to the next Starship test flight as soon as this week ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/13/spacex-cleared-to-fly-starship-again/); [SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/faa-provides-final-approval-for-next-starship-launch/)).
