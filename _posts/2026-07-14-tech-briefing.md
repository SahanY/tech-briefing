## Top Stories

**IBM blindsided the market with a preliminary Q2 warning that sent the stock down as much as 24% in premarket trading.** The company disclosed revenue of roughly $17.2 billion, up about 1% year over year but well short of its own internal plan, and adjusted earnings of $2.93 versus the roughly $3.01 Street consensus. Infrastructure revenue fell 7% on weak mainframe Z sales and the transaction-processing software attached to it, and CEO Arvind Krishna pointed to clients redirecting capital toward servers, storage, and memory ahead of expected supply constraints, plus large deals slipping past quarter-end. Final results and the earnings call are set for July 22 ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/ibm-preliminary-q2-results-miss-133359346.html), [StreetInsider](https://www.streetinsider.com/Corporate+News/IBM+misses+Q2+targets+as+mainframe+and+software+sales+disappoint/26764642.html), [Quartz](https://qz.com/ibm-stock-earnings-warning-second-quarter-revenue-071426)).

**A cooler-than-expected inflation print rescued an otherwise jittery tape.** June CPI rose 3.5% year over year against forecasts of 3.8%, and the relief lifted Nasdaq-100 futures roughly 1.3% as semiconductors bounced back from Monday's chip-led selloff. The move offset some of the drag from IBM and from renewed US-Iran conflict pushing oil prices higher, leaving the major indexes mixed-to-higher through Tuesday morning ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-july-14-2026), [24/7 Wall St.](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/07/14/live-nasdaq-composite-tech-stocks-rise-in-relief-treasury-yields-fall-as-cooler-inflation-gives-markets-a-tailwind/), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-tuesday-july-14-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-070833816.html)).

**Google is making its most aggressive enterprise-AI push yet with the Gemini Enterprise agent platform.** Positioned as the successor to Vertex AI, the platform bundles model selection, agent building, orchestration, governance, and security into one stack, alongside a no-code Workspace Studio, a 200-plus Model Garden that includes Anthropic's Claude, partner agents from Box, Workday, Salesforce and ServiceNow, and a $750 million partner innovation fund. Google says nearly 75% of Cloud customers now use its AI products and that its models process more than 16 billion tokens per minute, up from 10 billion last quarter — a direct shot at OpenAI's ChatGPT Work and Anthropic's enterprise offering ([Google Cloud Blog](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/the-new-gemini-enterprise-one-platform-for-agent-development), [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/google-cloud-next-ai-agents-agentic-era)).

## Enterprise Software

**Microsoft is doubling down on the services layer with Microsoft Frontier Company, a new consulting arm backed by $2.5 billion and 6,000 industry and engineering experts to help enterprises plan and deploy AI.** The move underscores an emerging thesis: with frontier models converging in raw capability, the differentiator is increasingly the deployment layer that turns a model into measurable P&L impact rather than the model itself ([HPCwire](https://www.hpcwire.com/bigdatawire/2026/07/06/microsoft-launches-new-2-5b-ai-initiative-with-6000-experts-to-help-enterprises-deploy-a/)).

**Microsoft's July 14 Patch Tuesday is unusually substantial.** The KB5095093 update for Windows 11 pairs the final stage of a year-long enterprise security overhaul with Point-in-time Restore, a new recovery feature that captures automatic system snapshots and lets a machine roll back to a working state after a bad driver or update ([Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Microsoft-s-July-2026-Patch-Tuesday-is-here.1341613.0.html)).

**The bottleneck for enterprise AI agents is trust, not capability.** Analysts note deployments stall less because models are weak and more because organizations fear agents leaking data, exceeding authority, or acting unpredictably — which is precisely why governance-first platforms and services engagements are drawing the spend ([ERP Today](https://erp.today/enterprise-software-faces-ai-driven-disruption-as-development-productivity-gains-fail-to-materialize/)).

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**Fundraising this week stayed concentrated in AI infrastructure and applied agents rather than consumer apps.** On July 13, Charlotte-based KredosAi closed a $7 million Series A led by BMW i Ventures, with Motley Fool Ventures and Walter Ventures joining; the company says it has processed more than 200 million customer interactions and grown revenue 6x while serving Fortune 50 clients. New York wealth-management fintech Arca emerged from stealth the same day with $64 million across seed and Series A to build an AI-powered platform ([The AI Insider](https://theaiinsider.tech/2026/07/13/kredosai-raises-7m-series-a-funding-round/), [Tech Startups](https://techstartups.com/2026/07/09/venture-capital-startup-funding-roundup-july-9-2026-sequoia-khosla-ventures-y-combinator-a16z-accel/)).

**The week's marquee round was Together AI's $800 million Series C at an $8.3 billion valuation.** The neocloud, which rents Nvidia GPU clusters and helps customers run open models like DeepSeek and Kimi at a fraction of closed-model cost, roughly 2.5x'd its valuation in a year as annual bookings crossed $1.15 billion. Aramco Ventures led, with Nvidia, Vista Equity, General Catalyst and others participating ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/01/neocloud-together-ai-raises-800m-leaps-to-8-3b-valuation/), [BusinessWire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260701243402/en/Together-AI-Raises-$800-Million-at-$8.3-Billion-Valuation-to-Make-Frontier-AI-Accessible-to-All)).

**Chipmaker and deep-tech rounds continued to dwarf software deals.** SambaNova Systems announced a $1 billion Series F first close at an $11 billion valuation, energy-infrastructure play Joulent raised $1.75 billion, and Norm AI closed a $120 million Series C led by Khosla at a $1.2 billion valuation, while Salesforce led a $135 million round for agentic-software startup 8090 Solutions ([Crunchbase News](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/biggest-funding-rounds-ai-energy-biotech-joulent/)).

## Tech Investing & Markets

**IBM's warning rippled across enterprise software peers.** Workday slid nearly 6% in premarket, Autodesk and Salesforce each fell about 3%, and Microsoft dipped under 2% as investors read the mainframe-and-memory shortfall as a possible read-through on software budgets tightening under rising component costs ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/ibm-preliminary-q2-results-miss-133359346.html), [Business Model Analyst](https://businessmodelanalyst.com/ibm-earnings-warning-memory-shortage/)).

**Semiconductors were the day's relative winners.** The VanEck Semiconductor ETF rose more than 2% premarket, with Applied Materials up over 4% and Lam Research, STMicroelectronics, Teradyne and Micron each gaining more than 3% as the CPI relief and firm memory pricing supported the group — extending a boom in which Micron and Nvidia are anchoring an estimated $700 billion chip profit cycle ([24/7 Wall St.](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/07/14/live-nasdaq-composite-tech-stocks-rise-in-relief-treasury-yields-fall-as-cooler-inflation-gives-markets-a-tailwind/), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/article/micron-and-nvidia-are-powering-a-700-billion-chip-profit-boom-chart-of-the-day-100000068.html)).

**The IPO window that reopened on July 1 is still trading well.** Bending Spoons — the acquisitive owner of Evernote, Vimeo, WeTransfer and AOL — priced at $29 and raised roughly $1.68 billion to fund more software buyouts, while micromobility operator Lime opened above its IPO price; both stocks caught a post-debut bid this week ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/01/bending-spoons-ipo-pricing), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/video/lime-and-bending-spoons-stocks-get-post-ipo-lift-general-mills-tops-q4-estimates-191926523.html)).

## AI & Emerging Tech

**The memory supply chain that IBM blamed for its miss is the same one powering the AI buildout.** SK Hynix began mass-production shipments of 12-layer HBM4 to Nvidia at the end of June and is ramping output, Samsung pulled forward its first Yongin fab to 2029, and SK Group's chairman signaled a US investment plan well beyond $35 billion — a reminder that AI demand and enterprise hardware are now competing for the same constrained components ([Digitimes](https://www.digitimes.com/topic/semiconductors/memory_chips/), [Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/07/09/samsung-and-deepseek-deliver-massive-news-for-nvid/)).

**On the research frontier, the week produced a few genuinely novel results.** Northwestern engineers built artificial neurons capable of communicating with real ones, and separate work showed that blending quantum computing with AI can sharply improve predictions of chaotic systems — early-stage but a signal of where model architectures may head next ([ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/computers_math/artificial_intelligence/)).

## Hot Takes & Commentary

**The uncomfortable subtext of 2026's layoffs is that AI is now the stated cause.** More than 160,000 tech roles have been cut this year, and 56% of layoff events explicitly cite AI or automation — including Microsoft's July 6 cut of 4,800 roles, with the Xbox division absorbing the bulk. The pattern of committing hundreds of billions to data centers while trimming headcount, and reinvesting the savings into chips and tooling, is becoming the defining tension of the cycle ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/22/the-running-list-major-tech-layoffs-in-2026-where-employers-cited-ai/), [Yahoo Tech](https://tech.yahoo.com/general/article/tech-layoffs-2026-2026-jobs-cut-144545528.html)). It's worth holding the counterpoint too: much of the reported "AI-driven" restructuring may be conventional cost-cutting wearing a more investor-friendly label.
