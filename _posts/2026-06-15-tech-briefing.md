## Top Stories

**Fox Corporation announced a definitive agreement to acquire Roku for approximately $22 billion, creating the third-largest U.S. television company by share of viewing.** Under the deal, Roku shareholders will receive $160 per share in a mix of cash and Fox Class A stock. The combination merges Fox's sports, news, and entertainment content — including Tubi — with Roku's connected TV platform and its 100-million-plus global streaming household base. Roku will continue operating as an open, partner-friendly platform. ([Fox Corporation](https://www.foxcorporation.com/news/corp-press-releases/2026/fox-corporation-to-acquire-roku-inc/), [Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/fox-acquires-roku-streaming-tech-deal-lachlan-murdoch-1236621853/))

**Salesforce signed a definitive agreement to acquire Fin, formerly Intercom, for approximately $3.6 billion in its largest AI-native acquisition.** Fin's core product is an AI customer service agent powered by its proprietary Apex model, which delivers industry-leading resolution rates across live chat, email, WhatsApp, SMS, phone, and Slack. The deal is expected to close in Salesforce's fiscal Q4 2027 and gives Salesforce a fast-deployment AI agent option particularly suited for SMB and commercial customers. ([Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/news/press-releases/2026/06/15/salesforce-signs-definitive-agreement-to-acquire-fin/?bc=HL), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-15/salesforce-to-buy-ai-customer-service-firm-fin-for-3-6-billion))

**Nvidia is preparing its first investment-grade bond offering since 2021, aiming to raise at least $20 billion across seven maturities ranging from two to 30 years.** The longest-dated tranche is being marketed at roughly 90 basis points over Treasuries. Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, and Morgan Stanley are managing the sale, with proceeds earmarked for general corporate purposes including debt refinancing. The offering follows a wave of mega-raises by Alphabet and Amazon to fund AI infrastructure. ([Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/nvidia-plans-to-raise-at-least-20-billion-in-bond-sale-4742033))

**The US-Iran peace deal announced over the weekend sent Asian tech stocks surging on Monday, with SoftBank jumping more than 12%.** Tokyo Electron rose 9.2%, Advantest gained 7.7%, Samsung added 4.5%, and SK Hynix climbed 6.4% as investors rotated into risk assets. Both sides declared an immediate and permanent cessation of military operations, with a formal signing ceremony set for June 19 in Switzerland. SpaceX continued its post-IPO rally, extending gains from its historic $75 billion debut on Thursday. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/softbank-up-more-than-12percent-on-news-of-iran-us-peace-deal-.html), [Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4603155-asian-tech-stocks-soar-softbank-leads-rally-on-us-iran-peace-deal))

## Enterprise Software

**1Password acquired just-in-time access provider Apono and simultaneously launched Credential Broker, a product that releases secrets to AI agents only when needed.** The moves position 1Password as a security gatekeeper for the fast-growing AI agent ecosystem, where identity and credential management for non-human workers is becoming a critical enterprise requirement. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/15/1password-acquires-just-time-access-provider-apono/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/15/1password-debuts-credential-broker-release-secrets-needed/))

**NinjaOne raised over $400 million in a Series C extension at a $12.3 billion valuation, more than doubling its $5 billion valuation from February 2025.** The Austin-based IT operations platform grew revenue over 70% in 2025 and posted a profit in Q1 2026. Wellington Management, Teachers' Venture Growth, Sequoia Capital, and ICONIQ participated. The capital will fund AI integration across every layer of its unified IT platform. ([NinjaOne](https://www.ninjaone.com/press/12-3-billion-valuation/), [Crunchbase News](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/biggest-funding-rounds-ai-biotech-healthcare-ninjaone-leads/))

**KPMG pulled its flagship report on agentic AI after UBS, the NHS, Swiss Federal Railways, and Transport for London said the case studies about their AI usage were fabricated.** GPTZero analysis found only 5 of 45 citations pointed to real, uncorrupted sources — a phenomenon the firm coined "vibe citing." The irony of an AI-about-AI report being riddled with hallucinations underscores persistent reliability risks in enterprise AI content generation. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/13/kpmg-pulls-report-on-ai-usage-due-to-apparent-hallucinations/), [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/06/12/kpmgs-ai-report-turns-into-a-demo-of-ai-hallucinations/5255029))

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**NewCore emerged from stealth with $66 million in seed funding to build security-first identities for AI agents.** As enterprises deploy autonomous agents that act as digital employees, NewCore aims to provide the identity infrastructure — authentication, authorization, and audit trails — that these non-human workers require. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/15/ai-agents-are-becoming-employees-newcore-emerges-with-66m-to-give-them-identities/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/15/newcore-launches-security-first-identities-ai-agents-closing-66m-seed-funding-round/))

**Mistral AI is in talks to raise approximately €3 billion ($3.5 billion) at a €20 billion valuation, nearly doubling its September 2025 Series C price.** The French AI lab secured $830 million in debt financing in March for a Paris-area data center and is investing in "physics AI" capabilities. ASML remains its largest shareholder from the prior round. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-12/france-s-mistral-in-funding-talks-at-about-20-billion-valuation), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/12/mistral-is-rumored-to-be-raising-e3b-at-e20-valuation/))

**Undo raised $37 million to give AI agents runtime debugging context**, while ChatSee closed $6.5 million for "failure memory" that helps enterprise AI agents learn from past mistakes. Both rounds reflect growing investor conviction that the AI agent stack needs dedicated tooling for reliability and observability. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/15/undo-lands-37m-give-ai-agents-runtime-context-fix-bugs/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/12/chatsee-raises-6-5m-build-failure-memory-enterprise-ai-agents/))

## Tech Investing & Markets

**SpaceX (SPCX) extended its post-IPO rally on Monday after debuting at $135 on Thursday and closing its first session at $161, a 19% gain that pushed its market cap past $2 trillion.** The $75 billion raise was the largest IPO in history and made Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire. Analysts at TD Securities see further upside, but some investors have called the valuation excessive. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/12/spacex-ipo-spcx-live-updates.html), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/12/nx-s1-5855004/stock-ai-spacex-ipo-elon-musk))

**Kazakhstan signed AI infrastructure accords with Nvidia-backed Firebird Inc. worth up to $10 billion.** The deal centers on "Data Center Valley" in northeastern Kazakhstan, featuring a 100,000-GPU cluster of Nvidia GB300 and Vera Rubin chips. Phase one involves $5 billion in investment including $1 billion from state-owned Kazakhtelecom. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-15/kazakhstan-firebird-ink-10-billion-ai-deal-with-nvidia-support))

**Meta is unwinding its $2 billion Manus acquisition after Beijing ordered divestiture on national security grounds.** Meta has cut Manus off from internal data systems entirely. The three Manus founders are exploring a $1 billion outside raise to fund a buyback at the original $2 billion valuation, with a potential restructuring as a Chinese joint venture and eventual Hong Kong IPO. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-11/meta-severs-manus-data-access-after-china-orders-buyout-unwound), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/13/meta-reportedly-moves-to-unwind-2b-manus-deal-after-beijings-demand/))

## AI & Emerging Tech

**The Anthropic export ban deepened over the weekend as the company disabled all access to its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models following a Trump Administration directive citing national security concerns.** The government acted after learning of a technique to bypass Fable 5's safeguards, though it provided no specifics. Anthropic publicly disagreed with the decision, calling it disproportionate to recall a model serving hundreds of millions of users over a narrow jailbreak. The move has intensified debate about US government control over commercial AI deployment and is driving enterprises toward "hardware sovereignty" strategies. ([Time](https://time.com/article/2026/06/13/anthropic-fable-mythos-ban-US-security/), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/06/13/anthropic-disables-fable-mythos-export-controls-national-security-threat/), [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access))

**OpenAI faces a sweeping investigation from a coalition of state attorneys general.** New York's AG served a subpoena seeking documents on advertising, user engagement, data handling, and activities related to minors and seniors. The probe adds to OpenAI's legal challenges ahead of its anticipated IPO, alongside a pending Florida lawsuit over ChatGPT safety representations. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/13/openai-faces-investigation-from-state-attorneys-general/), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-13/openai-probed-by-coalition-of-state-attorneys-general))

**The G7's draft communiqué singled out "potential risks" of AI, and AWS partnered with QuEra to lay out a two-year roadmap to fault-tolerant quantum computing.** ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-15/the-potential-risks-of-ai-singled-out-in-draft-g7-statement), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/15/aws-quera-lay-roadmap-fault-tolerant-quantum-computing-next-two-years/))

He called it Anthropic's "safety superpower": the ability to be trusted precisely because it cooperates even when it disagrees. ([Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/2026/anthropics-safety-superpower/))
