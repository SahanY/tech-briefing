## Top Stories

**SpaceX closed its first full day of Nasdaq trading up roughly 19%, cementing the largest IPO in history.** The company listed under ticker SPCX on June 12 after pricing 555.56 million Class A shares at $135, raising approximately $75 billion and landing a market cap above $2 trillion. MSCI announced early index inclusion eligibility starting today, creating structural buying pressure from passive funds tracking large-cap benchmarks — though the 4% public float means that demand is chasing limited supply.

**Roku stock surged 20% after Bloomberg reported the streaming platform is in acquisition talks with at least one U.S. media company.** No deal is certain, but the timing aligns with multiple tailwinds: Roku recently raised its full-year 2026 outlook after posting record premium subscription sign-ups, the company is being added to the S&P MidCap 400 effective June 22, and Evercore ISI lifted its price target to $185. The combination of M&A speculation, index inclusion, and improving fundamentals sent shares to $143.

**Adobe fell 6.5% despite posting record Q2 revenue of $6.62 billion and raising full-year guidance, after CFO Dan Durn announced his departure effective June 15.** The exit creates a leadership vacuum — Adobe is now entering the second half of its fiscal year without a permanent CEO or CFO, the two most senior operating roles simultaneously vacant. AI annual recurring revenue tripled year-over-year, but the market is pricing governance risk over growth metrics.

**Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei published a sweeping policy essay calling for FAA-style regulation of frontier AI models.** The proposal would grant the U.S. government standing legal authority to block or reverse deployments that fail independent third-party safety testing across four risk areas: cybersecurity, biological threats, loss of control, and automated R&D. Alongside the essay, Anthropic released two policy frameworks and committed $350 million in new funding to address AI-driven labor displacement.

## AI & Emerging Tech

**OpenAI's GPT-5.5 claimed the top spot on UC Berkeley's new Agents' Last Exam benchmark, edging out Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 with a 24% pass rate versus 22%.** The ALE benchmark, developed with over 300 domain experts, tests whether AI can execute real-world professional workflows — 3D modeling in Siemens NX, scene setup in Unreal Engine, neuroimaging analysis — rather than answering trivia. At the hardest difficulty level, the average pass rate across all models is just 2.6%, underscoring how far agentic AI still has to go.

**Apple wrapped WWDC 2026 with its biggest Siri overhaul ever, officially partnering with Google to power "Siri AI" with a custom Gemini model in a deal reported at $1 billion per year.** The redesigned assistant can analyze on-screen content, perform cross-app actions, and understand personal context through floating cards and contextual panels integrated into iOS 27. Apple signaled the Google arrangement is temporary — if its in-house models catch up, it can migrate Siri back to its own stack without changing the user experience.

**Researchers at a small lab claim to have trained a foundation model from scratch for approximately $1,500, challenging assumptions about the capital requirements for competitive AI development.** The work, reported by VentureBeat, suggests that algorithmic efficiency gains and open-source tooling are rapidly lowering the barrier to entry for model training, even as frontier labs spend billions on next-generation systems.

## Tech Investing

**Semiconductor stocks staged their biggest rally in a year, led by Arm Holdings (+11%), which hit new highs after Bank of America raised its price target to $335 from $245.** The catalyst is a structural shift in AI infrastructure spending: hyperscalers are now adding large volumes of CPUs alongside GPUs, and Arm's licensing model captures revenue from both. The Microsoft-Nvidia-Arm "New Era for PC" announcement at Computex added further momentum.

**Intel continued its remarkable 2026 comeback, trading up 6.5% and extending its year-to-date gain to roughly 250%.** Bank of America issued a rare double upgrade to Buy from Underperform, citing stronger server CPU demand from agentic AI workloads and improved Intel Foundry visibility after Google reportedly committed to having Intel produce over three million TPUs by 2028. Expanded CHIPS Act grants for Ohio and Arizona fabs have de-risked capex through 2027.

**Storage stocks Seagate (+7.2%) and Western Digital (+6.4%) rallied as AI-driven demand for high-capacity hard drives continues to outstrip supply.** Both companies have nearline HDD capacity fully committed through 2026 with long-term agreements extending into 2027. Seagate's Mozaic HAMR technology and Western Digital's 20% dividend increase reflect an industry riding the AI infrastructure buildout.

## VC & Fundraising

**Ramp raised $500 million as venture capital continues flowing to AI-adjacent infrastructure plays.** The broader funding landscape remains selective — AI captures roughly half of global venture funding — with the biggest checks going to companies near decision-making infrastructure, compute, and operational bottlenecks. AlphaSense, Oxford Quantum Circuits, and Coralogix were among the notable raises.

## Hot Takes

The SpaceX IPO is the market event of the year, but the 4% float creates a strange dynamic: passive index funds are being forced to buy a stock where almost no shares are available to trade. This is the kind of structural imbalance that makes for spectacular short-term moves and potential long-term pain. Meanwhile, Adobe's post-earnings drop is a case study in how leadership risk can override even strong fundamentals — tripling AI ARR doesn't matter when investors can't figure out who's running the company.
