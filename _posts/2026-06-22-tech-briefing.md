## Top Stories

**SpaceX launches inaugural bond sale, discloses $100.8 billion cash pile.** Ten days after its record-breaking $86 billion IPO, SpaceX unveiled a senior unsecured notes offering targeting at least [$20 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/22/spacex-spcx-bond-sale-ipo.html), with bonds maturing across five to 30 years. Moody's assigned Baa1, Fitch BBB+, and S&P BBB — all investment grade. Proceeds will retire bridge financing from the IPO. Shares fell 8% on Monday, pacing for a third straight session of losses and slipping [below Amazon in market cap](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/22/spacex-stock-ipo-rally-selloff.html) as early IPO euphoria fades.

**Alphabet slides 6.8% as AI talent exodus accelerates.** Google lost two of its most consequential AI researchers in a single week. Noam Shazeer, co-author of the "Attention Is All You Need" paper and Gemini co-lead, [left for OpenAI](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/google-gemini-co-lead-noam-shazeer-leaves-for-openai.html). Nobel laureate John Jumper, creator of AlphaFold, [departed for Anthropic](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/20/nobel-laureate-john-jumper-is-leaving-deepmind-for-rival-anthropic/). Investors piled on concerns about Alphabet's projected $180–190 billion AI capex for 2026 and the dilution from its $80 billion equity offering, sending the stock to one of its steepest single-day drops in recent memory.

**Meta taps CRED founder Kunal Shah to lead WhatsApp, invests $900M in the fintech.** Will Cathcart is stepping down after seven years leading WhatsApp. Shah, who built CRED into one of India's most prominent fintechs, will [replace him as global head](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/22/whatsapp-gets-new-chief-as-meta-taps-indias-cred-founder-kunal-shah-and-invests-900m-in-startup/). Meta is simultaneously taking a minority stake in CRED for roughly $900 million. India is WhatsApp's largest market with over 500 million users, and the appointment signals Meta's deepening push into business messaging and digital payments.

**Chevron and Microsoft sign 20-year, 2.7GW data center power deal.** Project Kilby, a co-located power facility in West Texas's Permian Basin, will deliver [approximately 2.67 gigawatts](https://www.chevron.com/newsroom/2026/q2/chevron-signs-20-year-power-agreement-with-microsoft-for-west-texas-data-center) of dedicated capacity to a Microsoft data center. GE Vernova turbines will supply the majority of generation. First power delivery is expected in 2028, with the project generating an estimated $10 billion in state and local tax revenue.

## Enterprise Software

**SpaceX signs $6.3 billion compute deal with Reflection AI.** The Nvidia-backed open-source AI startup will pay SpaceX [$150 million per month](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/22/spacex-ai-colossus-data-center-reflection.html) for access to GB300 chips at the Colossus 2 data center in Memphis, starting July 1. Either side can exit with 90 days' notice after three months. The deal cements SpaceX-xAI's Colossus as a commercial compute platform, adding to recent contracts with Anthropic, Google, and Cursor.

**Elastic reportedly acquires site reliability startup Deductive AI.** The deal, [reported by TechCrunch](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/19/elastic-reportedly-acquires-site-reliability-engineering-startup-deductive-ai/), gives Elastic AI-driven infrastructure error remediation capabilities, bolstering its observability stack.

**Klue supply-chain hack breaches multiple cybersecurity firms.** Hackers compromised Klue's OAuth tokens on June 11–12, [stealing Salesforce data](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/22/klue-hack-results-in-data-breach-at-several-cybersecurity-firms/) from customers including Huntress, HackerOne, Jamf, Recorded Future, Gong, OneTrust, and Snyk. The Icarus ransomware group claimed responsibility, threatening to publish stolen data. Salesforce has since disabled the Klue app integration.

**Tencent tests AI assistant Xiaowei inside WeChat.** Rather than building a standalone chatbot, Tencent is [embedding Xiaowei](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/22/tencent-ai-assistant-wechat-china.html) directly into WeChat's billion-user base. The assistant can initiate calls, draft messages, and navigate mini-apps, drawing on Weixin's LLM with DeepSeek as a fallback. A broader rollout is targeted for Q3.

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**Baseten raises $1.5 billion at $13 billion for AI inference.** Altimeter Capital, Conviction, and Spark Capital led the [Series F](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/ai-inference-provider-baseten-reportedly-raising-1-5b-funding/), more than doubling the company's $5 billion January valuation in five months. Baseten provides software and compute capacity for running inference, renting from roughly 20 cloud providers while layering its own optimization stack on top.

**Nearfield Instruments closes $380 million Series D at $1.6 billion.** The Dutch semiconductor metrology startup's round, led by Fidelity with participation from Temasek and QIA, is the [largest deep-tech raise in Netherlands history](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/22/semiconductor-manufacturing-process-control-startup-nearfield-instruments-raises-380m-accelerate-ai-chipmaking/). Nearfield's atomic force microscopes measure chip features just atoms tall, a capability in surging demand as AI chipmaking scales.

**Seedcamp raises $320 million for Fund VII, its largest ever.** The early-stage European investor is [expanding to the US](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/22/seedcamp-raises-320m-for-its-new-fund-to-expand-its-us-footprint/), splitting the fund into $220 million for seed-stage and $100 million for growth follow-ons. LPs include British Business Bank, HarborVest, and 80 portfolio-company founders.

**Other notable rounds:** Dream raised [$260 million](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/dream-raises-260m-sovereign-ai-cybersecurity-tools/) for sovereign AI and cybersecurity tools. Prem SA is seeking [$100 million Series A](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/prem-seeks-100m-series-export-bans-boost-sovereign-ai-demand/) at $500 million as export bans boost sovereign AI demand. Gradial grabbed [$65 million](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/agentic-marketing-ai-startup-gradial-grabs-65m-fresh-funding/) for agentic marketing AI. Architect Labs launched with [$24 million](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/architect-labs-nabs-24m-speed-chip-design-projects-ai/) for AI-accelerated chip design.

## Tech Investing & Markets

**Markets mixed; tech under pressure.** The S&P 500 dipped 0.15% and the Nasdaq fell 0.86% on Monday. SpaceX dropped 8%, Alphabet 6.8%, Amazon 4%, and Meta 3%. Micron bucked the trend, rising 4% ahead of its fiscal Q3 earnings Wednesday after the bell. Wall Street expects roughly $19.72 EPS on $34.5 billion revenue, with gross margins potentially [breaking 80%](https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/us-stocks/261979099-micron-q3-earnings-preview-gross-margin-expected-break-80-mark-tradingkey) amid the worst DRAM shortage in 15 years.

**Intel-Apple chip partnership.** President Trump announced that Apple will [design and build chips with Intel](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/trump-intel-apple-chip-design-deal.html) in the US. Intel shares jumped 10% on the news. The deal helps Apple diversify from TSMC, whose advanced fabs are oversubscribed with AI chip demand.

**Memory crisis intensifies.** Goldman Sachs pegged the 2026 DRAM supply-demand gap at 4.9%, the [most severe shortage in 15 years](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/19/memory-crisis-hits-such-extremes-that-even-apple-cant-be-safe-.html). Apple CEO Tim Cook called the situation "unsustainable," and the company appears poised to raise device prices. South Korea's chip worker bonuses have grown so large the [Bank of Korea issued an inflation warning](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/20/south-korea-tech-samsung-sk-hynix-bonus-bank-of-korea.html).

**SpaceX-Cursor $60 billion acquisition on track.** The [all-stock deal](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/spacex-spcx-cursor-acquisition-ipo.html) for AI coding startup Anysphere/Cursor, the largest VC-backed startup acquisition outside Musk's xAI self-deal, is expected to close in Q3. Cursor crossed $1 billion in annualized revenue in November.

## AI & Emerging Tech

**Anthropic export controls remain in effect.** Fable 5 and Mythos 5 have been offline for ten days after the Commerce Department's [emergency export control directive](https://www.nextgov.com/artificial-intelligence/2026/06/anthropic-suspends-top-ai-models-after-us-export-control-order/414173/) on June 12, triggered by jailbreak claims from a competitor. The order bars access by any foreign national worldwide, an unprecedented scope that has drawn scrutiny from [legal scholars](https://www.justsecurity.org/142745/law-anthropic-export-controls/) and [policy analysts](https://www.csis.org/analysis/department-commerce-restricted-access-anthropics-latest-models-what-comes-next).

**Nvidia launches Halos for Robotics.** The [industry's first full-stack robotic safety system](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-announces-halos-for-robotics-the-industrys-first-full-stack-safety-system-for-physical-ai) extends Nvidia's autonomous vehicle safety architecture to robots and physical AI. Agility is the first partner integrating Halos into its Digit humanoid robot. The Halos AI Systems Inspection Lab is ANAB-accredited for functional and AI safety certification.

**7,000 Langflow servers under active attack.** Check Point Research documented [critical vulnerabilities](https://venturebeat.com/security/7000-langflow-servers-under-attack-langgraph-langchain-same-holes) across LangFlow, LangGraph, and LangChain — three of the most widely deployed AI agent frameworks — with path traversal and SQL injection flaws enabling remote code execution.
