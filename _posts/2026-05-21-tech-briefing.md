## Top Stories

**Nvidia beats estimates but concedes China's AI chip market.** Nvidia reported record Q1 revenue of $81.6 billion, up 85% year-over-year, with data center revenue hitting $75.2 billion on surging Blackwell 300 demand. The company announced an $80 billion buyback and a 25x dividend increase. But the headline that moved markets came from CEO Jensen Huang, who [told analysts](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/nvidia-jensen-huang-china-ai-chip-market-huawei.html) that Nvidia has "largely conceded" China's advanced AI chip market to Huawei amid ongoing U.S. export restrictions. Huang also pitched a new $200 billion addressable market for CPUs powering AI agents, attempting to reassure investors who sent shares lower after a [forecast that disappointed](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/nvidia-nvda-earnings-report-q1-2027.html) relative to sky-high expectations.

**SpaceX files for the largest IPO in history.** SpaceX published its S-1, targeting a [Nasdaq listing under ticker SPCX on June 12](https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/us-stocks/261904604-spacex-ipo-spcx-date-set-for-june-12-175-trillion-valuation-tradingkey) at a valuation of $1.75 trillion or more, with a $75 billion raise that would dwarf Saudi Aramco's 2020 record. The filing revealed 2025 revenue of $18.7 billion, a $4.9 billion net loss, and [18,712 BTC on the balance sheet](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/05/20/elon-musk-s-spacex-holds-18-712-bitcoin-at-fair-value-of-usd1-29-billion-ipo-filing-shows) valued at $1.29 billion. Elon Musk retains 85.1% voting control through a dual-class structure and will not sell shares. The company is also [preparing a Starship test flight](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/spacex-set-to-launch-starship-rocket-in-key-test-of-ipo-plans) today ahead of the roadshow.

**Trump administration pledges $2 billion for quantum computing.** The White House announced $2 billion in grants to nine quantum-computing firms, [taking equity stakes](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/quantum-stocks--us-taking-equity-stakes.html) in each. IBM will receive $1 billion to build Anderon, America's first pure-play quantum chip foundry, backed by an additional $1 billion from IBM itself. GlobalFoundries is set for $375 million, while D-Wave, Rigetti, and Infleqtion could each receive roughly $100 million. Quantum stocks [surged across the board](https://www.zerohedge.com/markets/quantum-stocks-erupt-us-govt-awards-2-billion-takes-equity-stakes) in premarket trading.

**White House eyes executive order on pre-release AI model reviews.** A [draft executive order](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/20/ai-trump-executive-order-white-house-infighting) could be signed as early as today, establishing a voluntary framework for AI companies to share frontier models with the government before public release. The 90-day review window is being negotiated down — some companies are pushing for 14 days. The move follows Anthropic's disclosure of its Mythos model, which reportedly can exploit cybersecurity vulnerabilities at an unprecedented pace.

## Enterprise Software

**Intuit cuts 17% of workforce to restructure around AI.** Intuit is [laying off roughly 3,000 employees](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/intuit-to-lay-off-over-3000-employees-to-refocus-on-ai/) across TurboTax, QuickBooks, Credit Karma, and Mailchimp as it simplifies its corporate structure. CEO Sasan Goodarzi [denied the cuts are AI-driven](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/intuit-ceo-says-companys-17percent-workforce-cut-had-nothing-to-do-with-ai.html), though the restructuring will trigger $300–340 million in charges. The move comes as the company reckons with slowing growth across its core products.

**Microsoft and EY commit $1 billion to enterprise AI adoption.** The five-year initiative pairs [Microsoft Azure, Fabric, and AI agent frameworks with EY's consulting](https://news.microsoft.com/source/2026/05/21/ey-and-microsoft-announce-global-initiative-to-help-clients-scale-ai-enterprisewide-value-creation-and-move-beyond-experimentation/) and audit bench to help large organizations move AI from pilots to production. EY is also one of the first to adopt Microsoft 365 E7 and is scaling Copilot across its 400,000-person workforce.

**Workday expands AI agents into IT service management and travel.** Workday [announced new AI agents](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/21/workday-brings-ai-agents-service-management-travel/) that push the company beyond its HR and finance core into ITSM and corporate travel, signaling a broader enterprise agent buildout. Separately, Kore.ai [launched its Artemis platform](https://venturebeat.com/technology/kore-ai-launches-artemis-ai-agent-platform-expands-challenge-to-microsoft-and-salesforce), positioning itself as a challenger to Microsoft and Salesforce in the enterprise AI agent space.

**GitHub confirms breach of 3,800 internal repos.** A hacking group called TeamPCP [exfiltrated internal repositories](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/github-confirms-breach-of-3-800-repos-via-malicious-vscode-extension/) after a GitHub employee installed a poisoned Nx Console VS Code extension. GitHub says no customer data was affected. The attackers are attempting to sell the code for $50,000.

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**Hark raises $700M Series A at $6B valuation.** Brett Adcock's secretive AI hardware startup [closed a massive Series A](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/21/hark-raises-700m-series-a-for-its-secretive-universal-ai-interface/) to build a family of AI-native consumer devices. The company employs 45 engineers from Apple, Meta, and Tesla and is preparing its first model release this summer. The round is one of the largest Series A rounds ever.

**Mercury hits $5.2B valuation with $200M Series D.** The startup banking platform [raised from TCV, Sequoia, a16z, and Coatue](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/fintech-mercury-valuation-fundraise-bank-charter.html), a 49% valuation jump in 14 months. Mercury has reached $650 million in annualized revenue and four years of profitability. The company recently received conditional approval for a federal bank charter from the OCC.

**Fresha reaches $1B valuation with KKR backing.** The beauty and wellness booking marketplace [raised $80 million](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/21/booking-platform-fresha-announces-80m-investment-unicorn-valuation/) from KKR's Next Generation Technology Growth fund. Socket, a code security startup, [raised $60M at a $1B valuation](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/20/code-security-startup-socket-raises-60m-funding/) in a Series C led by Thrive Capital. Pivot Technologies [pulled in $40M](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/21/pivot-pulls-40m-push-agentic-ai-deeper-enterprise-procurement/) for agentic AI in enterprise procurement.

## Tech Investing & Markets

**Anthropic on track for first profitable quarter.** The Claude maker told investors it expects [Q2 revenue of $10.9 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/anthropic-revenue-explosive-growth-ipo-profitable-quarter.html), more than double Q1's $4.8 billion, with a projected $559 million operating profit. However, sustained profitability remains uncertain due to scheduled compute infrastructure cost increases. Separately, Anthropic is [in talks to use Microsoft AI chips](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/anthropic-in-talks-to-use-microsoft-ai-chips-information-says) and its Blackstone-backed consulting venture [acquired Fractional AI](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/anthropic-s-new-consulting-venture-makes-its-first-acquisition) as its operational hub.

**OpenAI reportedly preparing September IPO.** CEO Sam Altman is working with Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley and [may file confidentially](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/openai-barrels-toward-ipo-that-may-happen-in-september/) within days. The company also launched a [self-serve ChatGPT Ads Manager](https://ppc.land/openais-ads-manager-is-live-and-the-barrier-to-entry-just-dropped/) as it diversifies revenue. Both SpaceX and OpenAI IPOs coming this year would be [unprecedented for the tech sector](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/20/spacex-releases-ipo-filing-openai-reportedly-prepares-september-listing/).

**AMD commits $10B+ to Taiwan AI chip ecosystem.** AMD announced [investments across chip packaging and manufacturing](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/amd-taiwan-ai-chips-sector-10-billion.html) partnerships including ASE, Powertech, and Sanmina. Its next-gen EPYC "Venice" processor has entered production on TSMC's 2nm node, with the Helios AI server system due in H2 2026.

**Samsung rallies 6% as union suspends strike.** A [last-minute government-mediated deal](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/samsung-electronics-union-strike-suspended-wage-deal-bonuses.html) averted a planned May 21–June 7 walkout by 47,000 workers. The agreement includes performance bonuses tied to the chip division exceeding profit targets through 2035.

## AI & Emerging Tech

**Cohere releases Command A+ as first fully Apache 2.0 open model.** The 218B-parameter model [runs on a single Nvidia B200 GPU](https://venturebeat.com/technology/cohere-cracks-lossless-quantization-and-native-citations-with-first-full-apache-2-0-licensed-open-model-command-a) with only 25B active parameters per step, features native citation generation, and supports 48 languages. The release follows Cohere's [merger with German AI lab Aleph Alpha](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/25/why-cohere-is-merging-with-aleph-alpha/), backed by a $600M commitment from Schwarz Group.

**Google pitches an AI agent ecosystem to consumers.** Following I/O 2026, Google is rolling out [agentic commerce features](https://searchengineland.com/google-shares-whats-next-in-digital-advertising-and-commerce-in-2026-468995) including AI-powered shopping ads, Sponsored Stores in AI Mode, and "Business Agent for Leads" chatbots embedded in search results. The company also [confirmed ads will appear in AI Mode](https://blog.google/products/ads-commerce/google-marketing-live-search-ads/) search results.

**Tesla launches Full Self-Driving in China.** After years of regulatory delays, Tesla's FSD (Supervised) system is [now available in China](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/tesla-full-self-driving-china-launch-fsd.html) for 64,000 yuan ($9,410). The move follows Musk's Beijing visit alongside President Trump's delegation, though Chinese rivals Xiaomi and Xpeng have already expanded their own autonomous driving platforms.
