## Top Stories

**Micron Technology surged 18% to breach a $1 trillion market capitalization for the first time, after UBS nearly tripled its price target to $1,625 from $535.** The bull case rests on long-term AI memory supply agreements that stabilize Micron's historically volatile earnings, with UBS arguing the stock deserves Nvidia-like multiples. The revised target — the Street's highest among 46 covering brokerages — implies a potential $1.8 trillion valuation within twelve months, which would vault Micron past Tesla, Meta, and Berkshire Hathaway by market cap. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/26/micron-stock-trillion-market-cap.html), [Reuters](https://money.usnews.com/investing/news/articles/2026-05-26/micron-closes-in-on-1-trillion-market-value-as-ubs-triples-share-price-target))

**Qualcomm struck a deal to supply AI data-center chips to TikTok parent ByteDance, marking a major expansion beyond mobile processors.** ByteDance will procure millions of custom ASICs to power its AI agent software, making the Chinese tech giant one of Qualcomm's first large-scale data-center customers. Qualcomm shares jumped as much as 8.3% to a new intraday record. ByteDance has boosted its AI infrastructure budget 25% to 200 billion yuan ($29.4 billion). ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-26/qualcomm-strikes-ai-chip-deal-with-tiktok-owner-bytedance), [Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/qualcomm-shares-jump-following-report-of-massive-bytedance-ai-chip-order-4710327))

**Quantinuum, the Honeywell-backed quantum computing company, launched its IPO seeking to raise $1.05 billion at a $12 billion mid-point valuation.** The Broomfield, Colorado-based company is offering 21 million shares at $45–$50, with J.P. Morgan and Morgan Stanley leading the book. Quantinuum posted a $192.6 million net loss on $30.9 million in revenue for 2025, underscoring that quantum remains a pre-revenue bet for public-market investors. Bankers expect pricing next week. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-26/honeywell-backed-quantinuum-seeks-to-raise-1-05-billion-in-ipo), [IPOScoop](https://www.iposcoop.com/the-ipo-buzz-quantinuum-qnt-proposed-launches-1-billion-ipo/))

**Dropbox co-founder and CEO Drew Houston announced he is stepping down after 19 years, promoting GM Ashraf Alkarmi to co-CEO.** Houston will serve as executive chairman after a transition period. In an interview with CNBC, Houston said his next move will be entrepreneurial with AI as the focus — a telling signal about where veteran SaaS founders see opportunity. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/26/dropbox-ceo-drew-houston-ashraf-alkarmi.html), [Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4596855-dropbox-shuffles-executives-alkarmi-to-replace-co-founder-houston-as-ceo))

## Enterprise Software

**Novee debuted Agentic Fix, a security tool that routes pentest findings directly into Claude, Copilot, and Cursor for automated remediation.** The product reflects a broader pattern: security vendors are racing to embed agentic AI into developer workflows rather than building standalone dashboards. AppOmni similarly launched Marlin AI for SaaS security triage, and Detectify shipped an MCP server that lets AI agents discover and fix vulnerabilities in real time. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/26/novee-debuts-agentic-fix-pushing-pentest-findings-claude-copilot-cursor/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/26/appomni-launches-marlin-ai-automate-saas-security-investigation-remediation/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/26/detectify-debuts-mcp-server-let-ai-agents-find-fix-vulnerabilities-real-time/))

**Universal Music Group and TikTok signed a new multi-year licensing deal with expanded AI protections for artists.** The agreement includes commitments to remove unauthorized AI-generated music and improve artist attribution, putting behind a 2024 royalties dispute that briefly pulled UMG's catalog from the platform. The deal signals that Big Music is drawing firmer lines around generative AI while preserving the promotional value of short-form video. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/26/universal-music-group-and-tiktok-renew-agreement-to-combat-unauthorized-ai-music/), [TikTok Newsroom](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/universal-music-group-and-tiktok-announce-new-global-licensing-agreement))

**Spotify launched "Narrated Articles," bringing over 650 long-form magazine pieces from The Atlantic, Wired, Vogue, and Rolling Stone into its audiobook library.** Premium subscribers can access narrated articles within their existing monthly audiobook allowance; free users pay $1.99 per article. The feature uses a mix of human and AI-generated narration. ([Spotify Newsroom](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2026-05-26/articles-magazine-test-launch/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/26/spotify-now-lets-you-stream-narrated-magazine-articles-too/))

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**Crunchbase data reveals a widening gulf in early-stage funding: seed rounds are getting larger but Series A graduation rates have plummeted.** Only 24% of companies that raised a $1M+ seed round in 2024 have progressed to Series A, down from 55%+ through 2020. Meanwhile, over 40% of all seed and Series A dollars in 2026 have gone to rounds of $100M or more, concentrated heavily in AI. The median time from seed to Series A now exceeds two years. ([Crunchbase](https://news.crunchbase.com/seed/data-bigger-deals-longer-seriesa-2026/))

**The week's notable funding rounds included Hark ($700M Series A at $6B valuation), Armada ($230M Series B at $2B), and Mercury ($200M Series D at $5.2B).** Hark, a personalized AI intelligence lab, posted one of the largest Series A rounds in history. Mercury's round marked a 49% valuation jump for the digital banking platform. The IPO pipeline is also building: Zepto is planning a June filing for a $1B India IPO, while Crunchbase reports Q1 2026 global venture investment surged to $300 billion — nearly 70% of all capital deployed in 2025. ([Crunchbase](https://news.crunchbase.com/public/ipo-comeback-catch-exits-liquidity-declines-bercuson-earlyasset/), [TechStartups](https://techstartups.com/2026/05/21/venture-capital-startup-funding-roundup-may-21-2026/))

## AI & Emerging Tech

**Huawei unveiled a "LogicFolding" chip architecture based on a new Tau Scaling Law that prioritizes signal speed over transistor shrinkage.** The approach shifts from traditional node-driven scaling to system-level efficiency, optimizing data movement inside the chip rather than component size. Huawei claims to have secretly designed 381 chips based on the blueprint and plans a commercial debut this autumn with a new flagship Kirin processor. SMIC shares jumped 19% on the announcement. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/25/chinas-huawei-unveils-new-sanctions-busting-chip-architecture-replaces-moores-law/), [Bloomberg](https://www.bnnbloomberg.ca/business/international/2026/05/25/chinas-huawei-reveals-chip-design-breakthrough-amid-us-sanctions/))

**Pope Leo XIV released his first encyclical, "Magnifica Humanitas," a sweeping document on AI and human dignity.** The encyclical — presented at the Vatican alongside Anthropic co-founder Christopher Olah — warns against treating humans as objects to be perfected and calls for AI to serve "openness and communion." It extends Catholic social teaching from Rerum Novarum to the age of autonomous weapons and algorithmic manipulation. ([Vatican News](https://www.vaticannews.va/en/pope/news/2026-05/pope-leo-xiv-encyclical-magnifica-humanitas-ai.html), [Time](https://time.com/article/2026/05/25/pope-leo-encyclical-ai-magnifica-humanitas/))
