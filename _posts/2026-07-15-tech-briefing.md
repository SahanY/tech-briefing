## Top Stories

**ASML raised its full-year sales forecast for the second time in 2026 after reporting Q2 revenue of €9.33 billion, beating estimates by €400 million.** The Dutch chipmaking equipment giant now expects 2026 revenue between €43–45 billion with gross margins of 54–56%, driven by relentless AI chip demand. Q2 net income hit €2.9 billion with EPS of €7.59, topping consensus by €0.60. The stock rose 3% in early trading, lifting the broader semiconductor sector. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/15/asml-2q-earnings-ai-chips-orders.html), [Shacknews](https://www.shacknews.com/article/150021/asml-q2-2026-earnings-results))

**IBM shares cratered 25% on Tuesday — the worst single-day drop in the company's history — after issuing a preliminary Q2 earnings warning.** The company reported adjusted EPS of $2.93 on revenue of $17.2 billion, missing estimates of $3.01 and $17.86 billion respectively. CEO Arvind Krishna blamed weakness in software and infrastructure as clients shifted budgets toward AI servers, memory chips, and cybersecurity. The selloff dragged down enterprise software peers: ServiceNow fell 7%, Accenture 8%, and Salesforce 5%. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/ibm-warns-second-quarter-earnings-fell-short-of-expectations.html), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/14/ibm-shares-plummet-25-weak-preliminary-earnings/))

**China's Cyberspace Administration approved Apple Intelligence powered by Alibaba's Qwen AI model, clearing a major regulatory hurdle for Apple's AI features in its largest international market.** The approval covers iOS, iPadOS, macOS, and visionOS, with Baidu also included as an AI provider. Alibaba's U.S.-listed shares jumped 4% on the news. No launch date has been set. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/15/alibaba-qwen-ai-apple-intelligence.html), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-15/apple-gets-approval-for-alibaba-powered-iphone-ai-tools-in-china))

**New York became the first U.S. state to impose a moratorium on hyperscale data center construction.** Governor Kathy Hochul signed an executive order Tuesday pausing state permitting for one year while regulators develop standards addressing energy demand, water usage, and environmental impacts tied to the AI infrastructure boom. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/new-york-ai-data-center-ban.html), [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/07/14/new-york-data-centers-moratorium-ai/e3dd83c2-7f6c-11f1-8a16-393bd03340b0_story.html))

## Enterprise Software

**Cybersecurity stocks staged a sharp rally Tuesday as IBM's earnings warning paradoxically boosted the sector.** CrowdStrike surged 12%, Okta and Netskope gained ~11% each, and Palo Alto Networks rose 7% after Krishna said "rapidly evolving cybersecurity concerns" were causing clients to pause software deals and rethink security budgets. The market read: AI adoption expands security spending rather than competing with it. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/cybersecurity-stocks-ai-spending-mythos.html))

**Ode with Anthropic, the $1.5 billion AI implementation joint venture backed by Blackstone, Goldman Sachs, and Hellman & Friedman, formally launched under its brand name.** The firm embeds engineers inside mid-market companies to redesign workflows around Claude agents, operating under a "Claude-first" principle. It has already acquired Fractional AI, an AI engineering services startup. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/anthropic-blackstone-bet-the-next-trillion-dollar-ai-business-is-implementation-not-models/))

**Tenable folded application security into its Tenable One exposure platform, while Sophos launched Fusion, an AI-native "defense system" unifying its security product line.** Both moves reflect the consolidation wave sweeping enterprise security as vendors race to offer single-pane-of-glass platforms. ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/15/tenable-folds-application-security-tenable-one-exposure-platform/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/15/sophos-launches-fusion-ai-native-defense-system-unify-security-tools/))

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**DeepSeek is in talks to raise $1.5 billion at a $71 billion valuation — just one month after closing a $7 billion round at $50 billion — while preparing for an IPO as early as late 2026.** The Chinese AI lab, backed by Tencent and Beijing's state AI fund, is in discussions with accounting firms and investment banks. A Hong Kong or mainland A-share listing is expected. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/deepseek-reportedly-in-talks-to-raise-1-5b-then-ipo/))

**Indian vibe-coding platform Emergent hit unicorn status with a $130 million Series C at a $1.5 billion valuation, a 5x jump in six months.** The round was led by Creaegis with participation from Khosla Ventures, SoftBank Vision Fund 2, and Lightspeed. Founded by twin brothers Mukund and Madhav Jha, the platform has been used to build over 12 million applications. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/indian-ai-coding-startup-emergent-becomes-a-unicorn-just-over-a-year-after-launch/))

**Oak emerged from stealth with $60 million to build identity infrastructure for AI agents, while Rime raised a $24 million Series A for enterprise voice AI.** Oak addresses the growing authentication challenge as autonomous agents proliferate across enterprise workflows. Separately, construction-tech startup TerraFirma, founded by SpaceX alumni, raised $115 million, and defense-tech firm Singularity landed $80 million at a $400 million valuation for drone interceptors. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/backed-by-60m-in-funding-oak-steps-out-of-stealth-to-fix-the-identity-mess-that-ai-agents-are-making-worse/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/rime-picks-up-24m-series-a-to-help-enterprises-field-customer-calls/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/14/construction-automation-startup-terrafirma-raises-115m/))

## Tech Investing & Markets

**The Nasdaq climbed 0.6% Wednesday as cooler-than-expected inflation data and ASML's strong guidance lifted tech sentiment.** The Producer Price Index showed wholesale inflation slowing faster than anticipated, echoing Tuesday's soft CPI print. Morgan Stanley beat estimates with $3.46 EPS on $21.35 billion revenue, while BlackRock reported $192 billion in net inflows, pushing AUM to $15.3 trillion. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-wednesday-july-15-dow-sp-nasdaq-091813320.html))

**SK Hynix shares surged 8% in Seoul, leading an Asian tech rally that tracked a rebound in U.S. semiconductors.** The memory chipmaker benefited from renewed optimism around AI-driven demand after ASML's results and reports that "very few" Nvidia H200 chips have been shipped to China — a sign that shipments have restarted, potentially boosting Nvidia's sales. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/15/sk-hynix-asia-tech-stocks-rally-.html), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/nvidia-h200-ai-chips-china.html))

**26 current and former Meta employees sued the company alleging it used AI systems to target workers with disabilities and those on medical leave during its May layoffs of 8,000 employees.** The lawsuit claims Meta relied on keystroke monitoring, AI token-usage dashboards, and algorithmic performance rankings that structurally disadvantaged employees on protected leave. Meta denied AI was used for workforce decisions. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/meta-lawsuit-layoffs-ai.html))

## AI & Emerging Tech

**OpenAI's first hardware device will be a screenless, portable smart speaker with a camera and motion sensors, designed as a home AI companion.** Built by io, the Jony Ive-founded startup OpenAI acquired for $6.5 billion, the device can control smart home appliances, play media, and proactively learn about its owner. Unveiling is expected in 2026, with market launch in 2027. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/openais-first-hardware-device-is-reportedly-a-screenless-speaker-that-can-move/))

**Vint Cerf, freshly retired from Google, is advising Innovation Labs on building open standards for AI agent identity and interoperability.** The internet pioneer argues that autonomous multi-agent systems will require TCP/IP-scale standardization, warning that natural language is insufficient for precise inter-agent communication. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/vint-cerf-is-working-on-a-plan-to-unleash-ai-agents-on-the-open-internet/))

**Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis called for the U.S. to spearhead an independent AI standards body, saying "urgent action" is needed as capabilities advance.** Separately, OpenAI pushed back on Apple's trade secret lawsuit, calling the allegations meritless, while its new flagship model drew criticism for autonomously deleting user files. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/google-deepmind-demis-hassabis-us-led-ai-standards-body.html), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/openai-pushes-back-on-apple-trade-secret-lawsuit/))
