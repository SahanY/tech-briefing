## Top Stories

**President Trump announced that Intel and Apple have agreed to design and build chips together in the United States, sending Intel shares surging over 10% in early trading.** The deal would see Apple's M7 SoC manufactured on Intel's 18A-P process for MacBook Air and entry-level iPad Pro, with mass production targeted for late 2027. Apple has not confirmed the arrangement, and Intel declined to comment on "a potential Apple–Intel agreement." The announcement extends Intel's dramatic rally — the stock is up more than 200% this year — and raises fresh conflict-of-interest questions given the government's [existing 10% stake](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/trump-intel-apple-chip-design-deal.html) in the chipmaker.

**Noam Shazeer, co-author of the landmark "Attention Is All You Need" transformer paper and co-lead of Google's Gemini models, is leaving Google to join OpenAI.** The departure is a significant talent blow for Google, which paid roughly $2.7 billion in 2024 to bring Shazeer back from Character.AI. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman called Shazeer "one of the people I have most wanted to work with since the very beginning of OpenAI," calling the move ["only 10 years in the making."](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/google-gemini-co-lead-noam-shazeer-leaves-for-openai.html) The hire intensifies the AI talent war as frontier labs race to ship next-generation models.

**Accenture shares plunged as much as 20% — their largest single-day drop on record — after the consulting giant cut the top end of its annual revenue forecast and announced $4.2 billion in cybersecurity acquisitions.** The company is acquiring stakes in Dragos, runZero, and NetRise. But investors focused on the broader threat: AI automation is eroding demand for Accenture's traditional headcount-based consulting model. The stock has now fallen [nearly 48% over the past year](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-18/accenture-s-outlook-disappoints-in-uncertain-consultancy-market) and hit a 52-week low, making it a bellwether for AI's disruption of the professional services sector.

**Midjourney unveiled its first hardware product — a full-body ultrasonic scanner — and launched a new medical division.** The scanner submerges users in water and passes them through a ring containing half a million ultrasound emitters to produce whole-body imaging in about 60 seconds. Built on licensed [Butterfly Network ultrasound-on-chip technology](https://www.midjourney.com/medical/blogpost), the system targets a late-2027 spa launch with plans for 50,000 scanners worldwide by 2031. The pivot from AI image generation to medical hardware is one of the more unexpected expansions in recent tech history.

## Enterprise Software

**Adobe embedded agentic AI workflows across its entire Creative Cloud suite, shipping AI assistants into Photoshop, Premiere, Illustrator, InDesign, and Frame.io as a public beta.** The company also unveiled a redesigned Firefly AI studio with persistent context and reusable assets, signaling a shift from standalone media generation to [production orchestration](https://venturebeat.com/orchestration/adobe-embeds-agentic-ai-workflows-across-creative-cloud-shifting-from-media-generation-to-production-orchestration). The rollout positions Adobe to defend its creative franchise against a wave of AI-native competitors.

**Salesforce shares hit a 52-week low, down roughly 40% year-to-date despite reporting $11.1 billion in fiscal Q1 revenue (up 13% YoY).** The selloff reflects deepening market anxiety that AI automation will reduce demand for traditional per-seat SaaS subscriptions — the same dynamic hammering Accenture. Combined, the two stocks illustrate how [AI pricing disruption](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-18/ai-costs-what-shift-from-flat-rate-to-token-pricing-model-means-for-industry) is repricing entire categories of enterprise spending.

**Tesco is moving 40,000 server workloads off VMware, claiming Broadcom hiked its VMware prices by approximately 175% in UK court filings.** The retailer called it ["abusive conduct,"](https://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2026/06/tesco-moving-40000-server-workloads-off-vmware-amid-broadcoms-abusive-conduct/) adding to a growing list of enterprises fleeing Broadcom's VMware licensing changes.

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**XDOF emerged from stealth with $70 million to build infrastructure for training robots, backed by Thrive Capital, Andreessen Horowitz, Spark Capital, and Lux.** The company is already working with 20 customers including frontier AI labs, and released ABC-130K, what it calls the largest open-source teleoperation dataset, developed with [UC Berkeley, CMU, MIT, and Amazon](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/17/robotic-teleoperation-data-startup-xdof-launches-70m-funding/).

**Conduct AI raised $60 million in a Series A co-led by Index Ventures and ICONIQ to accelerate enterprise code modernization**, with SAP joining as a strategic investor. The platform automates legacy software migrations — a market that [expands as AI makes rewriting cheaper](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/17/conduct-raises-60m-speed-software-modernization-projects/) but still requires structured tooling.

**Convey closed a $38 million early-stage round led by Andreessen Horowitz for its agentic AI platform** that lets non-technical workers build autonomous digital teammates. The company targets [repetitive business process automation](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/17/convey-closes-38m-funding-round-help-companies-automate-repetitive-work-ai-teammates/) without requiring coding skills.

**BoolSi raised $6 million in seed funding to build a compiler that turns ordinary software into custom chips**, eliminating the need for specialized digital-logic training in chip design. The startup aims to [democratize ASIC development](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/18/boolsi-raises-6m-compile-ordinary-code-custom-chips/) as demand for custom silicon surges alongside AI workloads.

## Tech Investing & Markets

**SpaceX shares fell 5% on Wednesday, continuing to cool after a 49% surge in its first three trading days as a public company.** The stock briefly topped Amazon and Microsoft by market cap before retreating. Separately, Sequoia's Roelof Botha [joined the SpaceX board](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/spacex-adds-long-time-elon-musk-ally-roelof-botha-to-board.html), and California is counting on a potential IPO tax windfall from SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic — though experts say the revenue impact [may be blunted](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/california-ipo-tax-windfall.html).

**Bloomberg reported that Big Tech's AI spending spree is crowding out share buybacks**, one of the key forces that sustained tech stock valuations for years. As capex budgets swell, companies like Meta — which has pledged [$600 billion in AI infrastructure](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-18/big-tech-s-ai-spending-spree-is-making-stock-buybacks-disappear) over the coming years — are redirecting cash from shareholders to data centers.

**JPMorgan cut off access to Anthropic's Claude for its Hong Kong staff**, following Goldman Sachs, which did the same in April. The restrictions stem from Anthropic's licensing terms excluding Greater China usage, reflecting [tightening U.S. export controls](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4604629-jpmorgan-blocks-anthropic-ai-access-for-hong-kong-staff-report) on advanced AI models.

**Snap shares fell 17% over two sessions after launching its $2,195 AR glasses, Specs.** Investors are skeptical that Snap's core teenage demographic can support a premium hardware play, especially when [Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/after-unveiling-ridiculously-expensive-ar-glasses-snaps-stock-takes-a-dive/) retail for a fraction of the price.

## AI & Emerging Tech

**The Anthropic-White House standoff over Fable 5 and Mythos-class models continued to evolve, with co-founder Jack Clark confirming daily discussions with the Trump administration to restore access.** The Commerce Department ordered foreign access suspended last week after another company claimed it could [jailbreak Mythos](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-18/anthropic-lays-out-vision-for-how-to-bolster-ai-models-safety), marking the first time the federal government has forced an AI company to retract systems from public use.

**Yann LeCun called Musk's xAI "kind of a failure" and warned that AI labs face a "big bubble explosion" if they don't cut costs.** LeCun noted that xAI's founding team has departed, SpaceX's AI segment posted a [$2.5 billion operating loss in Q1](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/yann-lecun-elon-musk-xai-failure-ai-labs-bubble-risk.html), and the unit survives mainly by renting GPU capacity to third parties.

**NASA selected Eric Schmidt's Relativity Space for a Mars orbiter mission launching in 2028.** The Aeolus mission will carry instruments for the first daily global view of Martian atmospheric conditions, structured similarly to NASA's [commercial partnerships with SpaceX](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/nasa-picks-eric-schmidts-rocket-company-for-mars-mission-setting-up-a-race-with-spacex/).

**Waymo recalled nearly 4,000 robotaxis after identifying 13 instances where vehicles drove into closed highway construction zones.** The recall — Waymo's sixth — resulted in a full [suspension of highway operations](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/waymo-recalls-nearly-4000-robotaxis-to-stop-them-driving-into-highway-construction-zones/) while a software fix is developed.
