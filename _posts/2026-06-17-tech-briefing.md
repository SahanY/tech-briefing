## Top Stories

**SpaceX confirmed its $60 billion all-stock acquisition of Anysphere, the company behind AI coding assistant Cursor, just two days after its record-shattering IPO.** The deal — the largest AI developer-tools acquisition ever — gives Elon Musk's xAI division an immediate product with over a million paying users and $4 billion in annualized revenue. Cursor will become a wholly owned subsidiary upon expected closing in Q3 2026, as SpaceX races to compete with Anthropic and OpenAI in the AI coding tools market. SpaceX stock [slipped 5% on Wednesday](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/spacex-stock-elon-musk-market-cap.html), losing momentum after the post-IPO rally.

**AI CEOs including Sam Altman, Dario Amodei, and Demis Hassabis joined G7 leaders in Evian, France for a working lunch on AI deployment and safety.** The summit's final day focused on [the contentious future of AI and US dominance](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/g7-trump-ai-tech-leaders-openai-anthropic-google.html) of the industry. Mistral's Arthur Mensch, Salesforce's Marc Benioff, and a dozen other tech chiefs also attended. China, absent from the G7, simultaneously [pushed its own AI safety framework](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/china-pushes-for-ai-safety-as-g7-summit-wraps-up-without-beijing.html), seeking to counter Western governance norms.

**The fallout from the US government's order blocking Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models continued to dominate the AI policy landscape.** Anthropic [shut off access to both models for all users](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access) after Commerce Department officials cited a jailbreak vulnerability in Fable 5 that could enable cyberattacks. CNBC reported the shutdown is [a pivotal moment for open-source AI](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/anthropics-fable-shutdown-is-a-big-moment-for-open-source-ai.html), while TechCrunch noted sales data suggests the controversy [may actually be helping Anthropic](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/16/anthropics-latest-feud-with-the-trump-admin-may-actually-help-it-sales-data-suggests/) commercially.

**New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh holds his first press conference today at 2:30 PM ET following the June FOMC meeting.** Markets [expect rates held steady](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/federal-reserve-interest-rates-kevin-warsh-june-2026/) at 3.50–3.75%, but the updated dot plot and Warsh's comments on inflation — CPI hit 4.2% in May — will set the tone for H2 2026 rate expectations.

## Enterprise Software

**Databricks used its Data + AI Summit to launch Genie One, an agentic AI coworker that automates workflows across structured and unstructured enterprise data.** The product includes [Genie Agents and Genie App Builder](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/16/databricks-new-agentic-coworker-genie-one-brings-ai-automation-every-part-business/), all governed by Unity Catalog. Separately, Databricks announced the [acquisition of Panther](https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/databricks-agrees-acquire-panther-further-establishing-security), a cyberattack detection platform, marking its third security acquisition and deepening its "security lakehouse" strategy.

**SentinelOne opened Purple AI Agentic Investigation to all customers, enabling fully autonomous threat detection, investigation, and response.** The [zero-click capability](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/17/sentinelone-turns-purple-ai-loose-investigate-threats/) lets Purple AI investigate and act on threats without analyst intervention, with adjustable human-in-the-loop controls. The feature is available as a free trial inside the Singularity Platform.

**Pinterest launched "Ask Pinterest," an experimental AI shopping app exploring conversational, visual-first commerce.** The [standalone app](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/pinterest-launches-an-experimental-ai-shopping-app-called-ask-pinterest/) leverages Pinterest's Taste Graph and is available in limited US access. Pinterest also introduced an MCP for advertisers ahead of Cannes Lions.

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**DeepSeek raised $7.4 billion in its first external funding round, valuing the Chinese AI lab at over $50 billion.** The round was [led by Tencent (~$1.5B) and CATL (~$735M)](https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/articles/deepseek-eyes-7-4-billion-123039218.html), with a structure that preserves founder Liang Wenfeng's control via a limited partnership with a five-year lock-up and no voting rights for investors. The deal cements DeepSeek as China's most valuable AI startup.

**Atom Computing raised $300 million — including a $100M Series C led by Third Point Ventures and a $100M letter of intent from the US Department of Commerce — to accelerate fault-tolerant quantum computing.** The company [recently demonstrated quantum error correction](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/16/atom-computing-raises-300m-races-build-worlds-first-fault-tolerant-commercially-viable-quantum-computer/) on neutral-atom systems, a first in the industry.

**Pramaana Labs secured a $27 million seed round from Khosla Ventures to apply formal verification to AI systems.** The [funding aims to bring mathematically rigorous safety guarantees](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/pramaana-labs-raises-27-million-seed-round-from-khosla-ventures-to-bring-formal-verification-to-ai/) to AI model outputs, a growing priority as agentic AI deployments expand.

## Tech Investing & Markets

**Allbirds completed its transformation into AI infrastructure company Smartbird, appointing former AWS executive Nadia Carlsten as CEO and doubling its convertible financing facility to $100 million.** The stock [surged on the news](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/allbirds-bird-ai-smartbird-ceo.html), extending a five-fold rally since the pivot was announced in April. Carlsten brings experience from Alphabet spinoff SandboxAQ and Amazon Web Services.

**Man Group warned that AI credit markets face risk of a "violent" correction, urging investors to stay clear-eyed.** The hedge fund giant [flagged concentration risk](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/ai-credit-markets-at-risk-of-violent-correction-man-group-says.html) in AI-linked debt as infrastructure spending continues to surge ahead of proven revenue streams.

**Retail sales surged 0.9% in May, well above the 0.5% consensus, providing a strong consumer backdrop ahead of today's Fed decision.** Tech-weighted indices dipped slightly Tuesday on sector rotation but stabilized Wednesday morning as chips recovered.

## AI & Emerging Tech

**Z.ai fully open-sourced GLM-5.2, a 753-billion parameter model that has become the [leading open-weights model on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/glm-5-2-is-the-new-leading-open-weights-model-on-the-artificial-analysis-intelligence-index).** The model beats GPT-5.5 on multiple long-horizon coding benchmarks at roughly one-sixth the cost, with a 1-million-token context window and MIT license.

**DeepL acquired Mixhalo, a real-time audio streaming startup founded by Incubus guitarist Mike Einziger, to bolster its voice translation capabilities.** The [deal expands DeepL Voice](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/deepl-acquires-mixhalo-for-live-event-audio-streaming-and-translation/) into live events, conferences, and enterprise workflows, and establishes a new San Francisco office.

**Uber, Nuro, and Lucid announced Houston as the second market for their robotaxi program, targeting a mid-2027 launch.** The companies have [secured a 50,000 sq ft Houston depot](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/uber-will-bring-its-premium-robotaxi-service-to-houston-in-2027/) and will compete directly with Waymo, which already operates in the city.

## Hot Takes & Commentary

**Satya Nadella published a sweeping essay warning that AI risks "hollowing out entire industries" if value concentrates in a few frontier models.** His piece, titled "A frontier without an ecosystem is not stable," [argued that the political economy will not tolerate](https://venturebeat.com/technology/satya-nadella-warns-that-ai-could-hollow-out-entire-industries-echoing-the-damage-done-by-globalization) extreme value concentration — a notable framing from the CEO whose company is one of the largest frontier AI investors.

A WP VIP study found [60% of US consumers say "AI" in brand messaging is a turnoff](https://wpvip.com/future-of-the-web-2026/), complicating marketing strategies for companies rushing to slap AI branding on everything.
