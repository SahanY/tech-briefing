## Top Stories

**SK Hynix became the latest chipmaker to cross the $1 trillion market capitalization threshold, with shares surging as much as 11% on Wednesday as insatiable AI memory demand continues to reshape semiconductor valuations.** The South Korean memory giant, up roughly 250% year-to-date, has sold out its entire 2026 high-bandwidth memory production capacity through multi-year agreements with Nvidia and other hyperscalers. Analysts say the rally may only be halfway done, with earnings forecasts rising faster than the stock price. SK Hynix joins domestic rival Samsung Electronics, which crossed the $1 trillion mark earlier this month, making two Asian memory chipmakers now in the [trillion-dollar club](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/27/sk-hynix-shares-ai-chip-rally-1-trillion.html).

**Micron Technology also breached $1 trillion in market value on Tuesday, further cementing the memory chip sector's dominance in the AI trade.** The stock added another 4.75% on Wednesday after UBS raised its price target to $1,625, projecting earnings per share exceeding $100 through at least 2029. Like SK Hynix, Micron's entire 2026 HBM production is [fully allocated](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/27/5-things-to-know-before-the-market-opens.html) through long-term agreements.

**Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang announced plans to spend approximately $150 billion annually in Taiwan, calling it the "epicenter" of the AI revolution.** Speaking at a launch celebration for Nvidia's planned Taiwan headquarters at Beitou Shilin Technology Park, Huang noted spending has grown tenfold from $10–15 billion just a few years ago. The new headquarters will break ground this year, targeting 2030 operations with 4,000 employees, and bring Nvidia closer to TSMC. Taiwan chip stocks [rallied on the news](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/27/nvidia-taiwan-investment-150-billion-spending.html), while mainland Chinese chip names like Cambricon tumbled.

**AI coding startup Cognition raised more than $1 billion at a $25 billion pre-money valuation, more than doubling its value in eight months.** The maker of autonomous AI software engineer Devin now claims $492 million in annualized revenue run rate. Lux Capital and General Catalyst co-led the round, with participation from Founders Fund, 8VC, Ribbit Capital, and Atreides. The raise is a strong signal that top-tier VCs believe independent AI coding startups can [compete against model makers](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/27/ai-coding-startup-cognition-raises-1b-at-25b-pre-money-valuation/) entering the space.

## Enterprise Software

**Zscaler shares collapsed roughly 25% in pre-market trading after the cloud security firm guided fourth-quarter revenue below consensus, despite beating on every Q3 metric.** The company posted Q3 revenue of $850.5 million and adjusted earnings of $1.08 per share, both above estimates, but projected Q4 revenue of $875–878 million and slashed its full-year free cash flow outlook to 22.8–23.3%, down from 26.5–27%, citing increased capital expenditures and [sales leadership transitions](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4597354-zscaler-collapses-over-outlook-analysts-lower-targets-but-remain-upbeat-on-strong-nrr).

**Options traders are increasingly convinced the "SaaS-pocalypse" is over, with software stocks now up more than 25% from their April lows — the technical definition of a new bull market.** The iShares Expanded Tech-Software Sector ETF (IGV) has been climbing, though roughly $2 trillion in software market cap has been wiped out over the past twelve months on fears that AI agents will replace incumbent software companies. Whether the rally holds will [likely hinge on Salesforce earnings](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/27/new-bull-market-in-software-stocks-hinges-on-this-report.html) later this week.

**Amazon began licensing its AI-powered shopping technology to outside retailers through a new AWS product called the Agentic Shopping Assistant.** Tapestry's Kate Spade is an early customer, having deployed an AI Gift Concierge built on Anthropic's Claude Haiku via Amazon Bedrock. Retailers can customize the technology to their own inventory and identity [within 60 days](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/27/amazon-ai-shopping-alexa-kate-spade.html).

## Venture Capital & Fundraising

**Anthropic is closing a $30 billion-plus funding round at a valuation exceeding $900 billion, which would make it the most valuable private AI company in the world.** Sequoia Capital, Dragoneer, Altimeter Capital, and Greenoaks Capital are expected to co-lead, each investing roughly $2 billion. The round was expected to close [as soon as this week](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-22/anthropic-to-close-over-30-billion-round-as-soon-as-next-week), surpassing OpenAI's $852 billion March valuation.

**OpenAI confidentially filed its S-1 IPO prospectus with the SEC on May 22, targeting a Q4 2026 public listing at a valuation between $852 billion and $1 trillion.** Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley are leading the deal. Public financials should surface in August or September, with the listing window running [between Labor Day and Thanksgiving](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/27/ai-coding-startup-cognition-raises-1b-at-25b-pre-money-valuation/).

**Thea Energy, the Princeton-based fusion startup, raised $100 million to become one of the top-funded fusion companies.** The company's pixel-inspired magnet technology aims to get a commercial reactor working [by 2034](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/27/with-a-new-100m-raise-princetons-thea-energy-is-now-a-top-funded-fusion-startup/). Separately, Capchase, a B2B vendor financing platform, secured [$200 million in debt and equity](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/fintech-capchase-b2b-bnpl-200m-debt-equity/) led by 01 Advisors, reporting 400% growth over the past twelve months.

## AI & Emerging Tech

**CrowdStrike and Google executed a coordinated takedown of the Glassworm botnet, a global supply chain threat targeting software developers.** The operation severed four command-and-control channels — including Solana blockchain transaction memos and Google Calendar dead drops — used by likely Russian operators who had poisoned over 300 GitHub repositories, trojanized VSCode extensions, and compromised npm and Python packages. The cross-platform malware affected Windows, macOS, and Linux systems with credential harvesting and [remote access capabilities](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/27/crowdstrike-and-google-take-down-botnet-used-by-hackers-to-target-software-developers-in-supply-chain-attacks/).

**ElevenLabs launched a music-generation model capable of switching genres mid-track, letting users regenerate song sections without affecting the rest.** The release intensifies competition in generative audio as the company [expands beyond voice synthesis](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/27/elevenlabss-new-music-generation-model-can-switch-genres-mid-track/). China is also increasingly retaining its top AI talent domestically, with Beijing growing reluctant to let world-class researchers leave as its [domestic AI boom accelerates](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/27/china-is-increasingly-keeping-its-best-ai-talent-to-itself/).
